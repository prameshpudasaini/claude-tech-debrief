# 2026-07-19

- **Anthropic files a confidential S-1, setting up a possible IPO as soon as October.** The Claude maker filed with the SEC after closing its Series H at a $965 billion valuation in June; secondary-market trading and analyst estimates now put a potential IPO valuation as high as $1.2–1.8 trillion, which would make it the largest tech listing ever. Revenue run-rate has reportedly crossed $47 billion annualized.
- **Apple overtook Nvidia as the world's most valuable company on Friday.** Apple closed at a $4.88 trillion market cap versus Nvidia's ~$4.86 trillion, its first time back on top since April 2025. The rotation reflects investors favoring AI "distribution and monetization" plays (Apple, up 22% YTD) over pure infrastructure names as capex-payoff doubts creep in.
- **Hyundai Motor Group is closing in on 100% ownership of Boston Dynamics, fueling Nasdaq IPO speculation.** Executive Chair Chung Euisun is raising his personal stake to 25% and the group is buying out SoftBank's remaining ~9.9–9.65% stake for roughly $325 million; full consolidation is seen as a precursor to a future robotics listing that could value the company north of $100 trillion won (~$72B+).
- **DeepSeek is reportedly seeking to raise $70 billion+ at a $74 billion valuation** ahead of a planned Shanghai listing next year, while also developing its own AI chip to cut reliance on Nvidia and Huawei — another sign Chinese AI labs are racing toward public markets and hardware independence simultaneously.
- **Chip stocks just had their worst week since April 2025.** The Philadelphia Semiconductor Index (SOX) sank about 10% last week as investors questioned whether AI infrastructure capex will pay off, dragging Nvidia, AMD, Micron, Broadcom and Qualcomm lower even as Apple and other "AI application layer" names held up better.
- **SpaceX shares closed below their $135 IPO price for the first time since the June listing.** The stock has lost roughly 45% from its post-IPO peak and erased over $1.2 trillion in market value, pressured by a disclosed $4.9 billion annual net loss and a money-losing xAI unit weighing on an otherwise-profitable Starlink business.

The past few sessions show a rotation story taking shape: capital is drifting away from "picks and shovels" AI infrastructure (Nvidia, chip-equipment names, SpaceX post-IPO) toward companies that monetize AI through distribution and services (Apple), while a wave of AI-adjacent IPOs — Anthropic, Boston Dynamics, DeepSeek's eventual Shanghai listing — lines up for the second half of the year. Watch AAPL and NVDA for continued tug-of-war at the top of the market-cap table, keep SOX/MU/AVGO/QCOM on radar for further capex-doubt selling, and treat any pre-IPO secondary pricing on Anthropic as a read-through for how investors are valuing the broader foundation-model sector.
