# 2026-06-28 — Tech Deals & Market Movers

---

- **SpaceX acquires xAI for $250B:** In the largest corporate acquisition of 2026, SpaceX agreed to take over Elon Musk's AI company xAI, folding it into the same entity that already controls Starship, Starlink, and the recently acquired Cursor coding assistant. The deal consolidates Musk's AI, infrastructure, and space assets under a single $2T+ umbrella — watch **SPCX**, which now holds one of the densest concentrations of AI assets of any public company.

- **IonQ/SkyWater merger wins shareholder approval:** SkyWater Technology stockholders voted to approve the $1.8B cash-and-stock merger with quantum computing firm IonQ, clearing a key regulatory milestone. The deal — $15 cash plus $20 in IonQ stock per share — creates the only vertically integrated quantum computing company with its own semiconductor foundry; closure is expected Q2–Q3 2026. Watch **IONQ** and **SKYT**.

- **AMD and Meta sign 6-gigawatt GPU supply agreement:** AMD and Meta announced a multi-year, multi-generation partnership to deploy up to 6GW of AMD Instinct GPUs across Meta's AI data centers, with the first gigawatt of shipments beginning in 2H 2026. The deal is AMD's largest disclosed compute contract and a direct challenge to Nvidia's near-monopoly on AI accelerator supply. Watch **AMD** and **META**.

- **Electronic Arts take-private advancing toward close:** The $55B buyout of EA by PIF, Silver Lake, and Affinity Partners — the largest all-cash sponsor take-private in history at $210/share — is on track to close in Q1 FY2027 following shareholder approval in December 2025. Regulatory reviews remain the key gating item; EA continues trading near the buyout price. Watch **EA**.

- **SharonAI Holdings signs six-year, 72MW Nvidia AI factory deal:** SharonAI Holdings inked a six-year compute collaboration with Nvidia to deploy 40,000 Grace Blackwell GB300 GPUs across a 72MW AI factory in Australia — one of the largest Nvidia AI factory commitments outside the United States. The deal adds to a growing list of international hyperscale GPU deployments anchored to Nvidia's GB300 platform. Watch **NVDA**.

- **Lime (Neutron Holdings) sets Nasdaq IPO terms at $24–$26/share:** The e-scooter and micromobility operator updated its IPO share range to $24–$26 for approximately 7 million shares, implying a listing valuation in the low billions. The deal tests investor appetite for consumer mobility plays in a market dominated by AI listings. Watch the upcoming **Neutron Holdings** debut.

- **Big Tech AI capex on pace for $650B in 2026:** Annual capital expenditure commitments from the four hyperscalers — Amazon (~$200B), Alphabet ($175–185B), Meta ($115–135B), and Microsoft (~$145B run rate) — put collective 2026 AI infrastructure spending on track for $650B. The scale of spend keeps pressure on the entire AI supply chain, from chip makers to data center REITs. Watch **NVDA**, **AMD**, **EQIX**, **DLR**.

## What It Means

This weekend's deal activity is defined by vertical integration and the accelerating consolidation of AI assets. SpaceX's $250B xAI acquisition is the clearest signal yet that the most valuable AI companies are no longer standalone — they are being absorbed into vertically integrated conglomerates that control chips, data centers, models, and applications in one stack. The IonQ/SkyWater stockholder vote and AMD's 6GW Meta pact reinforce the same theme: owning the full hardware-to-software pipeline is the new competitive moat. For traders, the AMD/Meta deal is the most near-term actionable — AMD's credibility as an Nvidia alternative just received a massive vote of confidence from one of the world's largest AI spenders, and with 1GW of deliveries starting in 2H 2026, revenue impact is quarters away, not years. Keep an eye on the Lime IPO as a sentiment gauge for non-AI public offerings, and watch the EA situation for any regulatory surprise that could move the stock off its $210 buyout floor.
