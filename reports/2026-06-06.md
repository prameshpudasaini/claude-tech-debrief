# June 6, 2026 — Tech Market Debrief

## Major Deals & Moves (Past 24 Hours)

- **SpaceX IPO roadshow underway (SPCX, Nasdaq):** SpaceX's roadshow runs June 4–8, with pricing expected June 11 and first trading June 12. The company is targeting a $1.75T valuation and aims to raise up to $75B — which would make it the largest IPO in history.

- **Anthropic files confidentially for IPO:** Anthropic submitted a confidential S-1 filing on June 1, days after closing a $65B Series H round that pushed its valuation to ~$965B. With run-rate revenue crossing $47B and its first projected operating profit, Anthropic is now the world's most valuable private AI startup.

- **Nvidia signs $20B licensing deal with Groq:** Nvidia agreed to license AI inference chip technology from startup Groq and bring on its executive team in a deal valued at $20B. The move further consolidates Nvidia's position across both training and inference in the AI chip stack.

- **Meta takes 49% stake in Scale AI for $14.3B:** Meta acquired a near-majority position in Scale AI, the dominant AI data labeling and training infrastructure platform. The deal gives Meta a strategic lock on the data pipelines that underpin model development at scale.

- **AMD and Meta expand GPU partnership to 6 gigawatts:** AMD and Meta formalized a multi-year, multi-generation agreement to deploy up to 6GW of AMD Instinct GPUs, with the first 1GW deployment beginning in H2 2026. The deal is a significant win for AMD in the hyperscaler AI hardware race.

- **BlackRock/MGX consortium acquires Aligned Data Centers for $40B:** The private infrastructure consortium closed one of the largest data center deals in history, reflecting soaring demand for AI-ready compute capacity as hyperscalers race to expand.

- **DOJ clears Google's acquisition of Wiz:** The Department of Justice approved Alphabet's acquisition of cloud security firm Wiz, removing the last major regulatory hurdle and giving Google Cloud a significant edge in enterprise security.

- **Quantinuum goes public (June 4):** Quantum computing company Quantinuum priced and began trading June 4 in one of the first high-profile quantum computing IPOs, drawing investor attention to the emerging sector.

## Market Synthesis

The week's deals tell a single story: the AI infrastructure buildout is accelerating, not plateauing. From chips (Nvidia–Groq, AMD–Meta) to data pipelines (Meta–Scale AI) to physical compute (BlackRock–Aligned Data Centers), every layer of the AI stack is being locked up through acquisitions and long-term partnerships. The SpaceX and Anthropic IPO pipelines signal that the public markets window is wide open for marquee names, and both listings could inject significant sentiment into growth-tech broadly. Stocks to watch: **NVDA** (Groq licensing bolsters inference leadership), **AMD** (6GW Meta commitment is a revenue catalyst), **GOOGL** (Wiz integration clears the way), and **META** (Scale AI stake secures training data moat). The $650B in combined hyperscaler capex commitments for 2026 makes clear that AI spending is structural, not cyclical — any dip in infrastructure-adjacent names remains a buy-the-dip setup.
