# 2026-06-25 — Tech Deals & Market Movers

---

- **Qualcomm acquires Modular for ~$4B (June 24):** Qualcomm announced an all-stock deal to buy AI software startup Modular, which built the Mojo programming language and MAX AI platform — software that runs AI workloads across Nvidia, AMD, and Intel chips without rewriting code. The move is a direct shot at Nvidia's CUDA software moat and could make Qualcomm's own chips more attractive to developers.

- **SpaceX signs $6.3B compute deal with Reflection AI (June 24):** Nvidia-backed AI startup Reflection AI agreed to pay SpaceX $150 million per month starting July 1 for access to Nvidia GB300 chips inside SpaceX's Colossus 2 data center in Memphis. The deal underscores SpaceX's rapid emergence as a major AI infrastructure player, just weeks after its record IPO.

- **SpaceX acquires Cursor (Anysphere) for $60B (June 16 — still being priced in):** Six days after its $75B Nasdaq IPO, SpaceX announced the largest-ever acquisition of a venture-backed startup — buying AI coding assistant Cursor in an all-stock deal. Cursor hit ~$4B in annualized revenue in under four years. SPCX shares surged to ~$192 on the news before pulling back slightly.

- **Anthropic files confidentially for IPO:** The Claude-maker filed for a public offering after closing a $65B Series H round that valued the company at ~$965B. Anthropic has not set a timeline, but the filing signals another potential mega-cap AI listing on the horizon after SpaceX's blockbuster debut.

- **Google DeepMind invests $75M in A24 (June 22):** Google DeepMind took its first-ever equity stake in a film studio, partnering with A24 to embed AI researchers inside active film productions. The initial tool in development is an AI storyboard generator — Google's bet on AI becoming a core creative-industry tool.

- **Meta + CoreWeave expand AI cloud deal to $21B through 2032:** CoreWeave announced an expanded long-term agreement with Meta to supply AI cloud capacity through December 2032. The deal cements CoreWeave's position as a critical AI infrastructure provider and adds revenue visibility well into the decade.

- **Superhuman acquires GPTZero (June 24):** Email productivity platform Superhuman agreed to acquire GPTZero, the AI-detection startup founded by Edward Tian that identifies whether content was written by a human or AI. The deal reflects growing demand for AI-authenticity tools as AI-generated content floods enterprise workflows.

## What It Means

The week's deal activity is defined by two themes: the rise of SpaceX as a $2.7T+ dealmaking force reshaping the AI infrastructure and software landscape, and a broad push by chip and platform companies to break Nvidia's software grip. Qualcomm's $4B Modular acquisition is the clearest signal yet that the hardware wars are evolving into a software and developer-ecosystem battle — watch **QCOM** and **NVDA** for reaction. SpaceX's $6.3B Reflection AI compute agreement shows the newly public **SPCX** is already a hyperscaler-in-the-making, with Meta's multi-billion CoreWeave extension highlighting **CRWV** as a durable AI infrastructure play. With Anthropic's IPO filing now confirmed, the new-issue window stays wide open — investors should track the broader AI software IPO pipeline as the next catalyst.
