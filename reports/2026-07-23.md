# July 23, 2026 — Tech & Markets Morning Debrief

- **AMD to invest up to $5B in Anthropic.** As part of a broader computing-power partnership, AMD will deploy 2 gigawatts of its Instinct MI450 GPUs for Anthropic starting in early 2027, with the equity investment tied to hitting deployment milestones — a direct shot at Nvidia's grip on AI training hardware. (AMD, announced July 22)
- **Hut 8 fully commercializes its Texas AI campus with a second $9.8B lease.** The 15-year deal brings total contracted value at the Beacon Point site to $19.6B and pushed Hut 8 shares up over 12% — the latest sign that ex-bitcoin miners are cashing in as AI data-center landlords.
- **Alphabet hikes 2026 capex guidance to $195B–$205B, spooking investors.** Despite a Q2 beat and Google Cloud revenue up 82% YoY to $24.8B, shares fell nearly 5% after the company posted its first-ever negative free cash flow quarter — a reminder that AI infrastructure spending is starting to bite margins across the hyperscalers.
- **Qualcomm reportedly weighing a bid for parts of Intel's chip design business.** Reuters sources say Qualcomm has examined acquiring select Intel units, following its ~$4B purchase of AI-software startup Modular in June — Qualcomm is clearly building out a bigger data-center and AI chip stack.
- **SpaceX's record $60B acquisition of Cursor-maker Anysphere still on track to close in Q3.** The largest AI-startup acquisition ever remains a name to watch as it clears regulatory review, folding a leading AI coding tool into SpaceX's enterprise AI ambitions.
- **Nvidia's $4B stake in AI-cloud provider Nebius keeps paying off.** Shares advanced on continued disclosure of the investment, underscoring Nvidia's strategy of seeding capacity across multiple AI-cloud partners rather than relying on hyperscalers alone.

Today's moves all point to one theme: **AI infrastructure spending is the market's swing factor.** Chipmakers (AMD, Nvidia, Qualcomm) are trading equity stakes for guaranteed demand, data-center operators (Hut 8) are riding a leasing boom, and hyperscalers (Alphabet) are getting punished short-term for capex that investors worry won't pay off fast enough. Watch AMD and Hut 8 for continued upside momentum, and keep an eye on Alphabet, Meta, and Amazon earnings this week — capex guidance, not revenue beats, is what's moving these stocks. Chip-sector consolidation chatter (Qualcomm/Intel) is also one to track for a potential surprise deal.
