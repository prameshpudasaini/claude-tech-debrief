# July 1, 2026

- **Getty Images terminates $3.7B Shutterstock merger.** Getty's board voted to let the deal lapse after the UK Competition and Markets Authority demanded Shutterstock sell off its editorial business as a condition of approval — a condition Getty wasn't required to accept. Shutterstock shares plunged over 30% premarket; Getty will now redeem its senior notes and explore standalone financing options.
- **Meta is building a new cloud business ("Meta Compute") to sell excess AI computing power**, per a Bloomberg report — one arm would offer model access akin to AWS Bedrock, the other raw compute capacity like a neocloud (CoreWeave-style). Meta shares jumped roughly 9-11%, as the move pits Meta directly against AWS, Azure, and Google Cloud.
- **Palantir extended its rally on a Nvidia sovereign-AI partnership plus a new Surf Air Mobility commercial deal.** The Nvidia tie-up will embed Nemotron open-source models into Palantir's AIP, Foundry, Ontology, and Apollo platforms for government and critical-infrastructure customers; Palantir stock rose over 7%.
- **AeroVironment shares surged ~19% after blockbuster fiscal Q4 earnings**, with revenue up 133% year-over-year to $641.6 million and backlog growing 65% to $1.2 billion, driven by surging drone demand tied to defense spending.
- **TSMC and Amkor Technology solidified a 10-year advanced packaging and testing partnership** to expand capacity in Arizona, deepening TSMC's US-based manufacturing footprint amid ongoing reshoring pressure.
- **IQM Quantum Computers completes its SPAC merger**, set to begin Nasdaq trading July 2 — a rare pure-play, publicly traded quantum-hardware name entering the market.
- **SpaceX is reportedly in high-level talks with Charter Communications** on a consumer-facing mobile partnership, adding speculative fuel to SpaceX shares ahead of its July 7 entry into the Nasdaq-100.

The dominant theme remains AI infrastructure economics: Meta's compute-reselling pivot shows even the biggest AI spenders are looking to monetize excess capacity, while chip and memory suppliers (Micron, Intel, AMD, Marvell, SanDisk) keep adding market cap as "picks-and-shovels" plays on constrained supply — a trend that added roughly $2 trillion in combined value to that group last quarter alone. Meanwhile, the collapsed Getty-Shutterstock deal is a reminder that regulatory risk, especially from UK/EU antitrust bodies, can still sink even modest-sized tech consolidation. Watch META, PLTR, AVAV, TSM, MU, INTC, AMD, and MRVL for near-term momentum, and GETY/SSTK for post-breakup volatility.
