# June 9, 2026 — Tech Market Debrief

## Today's Major Deals & Moves

- **OpenAI files confidential S-1 (June 8):** OpenAI submitted a confidential IPO prospectus to the SEC on June 8, targeting a valuation between $852 billion and $1 trillion and eyeing a September 2026 Nasdaq listing — following rival Anthropic, which filed its own S-1 just one week earlier on June 1.

- **Anthropic confidential IPO filing (June 1):** Anthropic filed confidentially for an IPO at a ~$965 billion valuation, days after closing a $65 billion Series H round; the company reported a $47 billion annualized revenue run rate and is on track for its first profitable quarter.

- **SpaceX prices IPO at $135/share, debuts June 12:** SpaceX set its offering price at $135 per share, implying a $1.77 trillion valuation, with 555.6 million shares sold for a ~$75 billion raise — the largest IPO in history. It lists on Nasdaq under ticker **SPCX** on June 12.

- **Apple + Google/Anthropic/OpenAI: Siri goes multi-model (June 8):** At WWDC 2026, Apple announced that Siri is now powered by Google's Gemini and will support opt-in extensions for ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini inside iOS 27, iPadOS 27, and macOS 27 — deepening Apple's AI partnerships across multiple competitors.

- **NVIDIA + TSMC AI fab partnership (June 1):** NVIDIA and TSMC announced a manufacturing partnership to embed Nvidia's CUDA-X AI stack directly into TSMC's chip fabs, targeting faster yields, better defect detection, and improved wafer scheduling — announced at GTC Taipei.

- **Honeywell aerospace spinoff on track for June 29:** Honeywell confirmed its aerospace division will spin off as **Honeywell Aerospace (HONA)** on Nasdaq on June 29, with a record date of June 15. HON shareholders receive one HONA share for every two HON shares held, creating two independent publicly traded companies.

- **Lime files for Nasdaq IPO under "LIME":** Electric scooter and bike startup Lime filed for a public offering in May, targeting a Nasdaq listing in June 2026 — part of a broader consumer-tech return to public markets.

---

## What It Means for the Market

The defining story this week is the AI IPO flood: OpenAI, Anthropic, and SpaceX together represent potentially $3.5+ trillion in new listings hitting public markets over the next few months. This wave of new supply could pull capital away from existing AI-adjacent names like **Nvidia (NVDA)**, **Microsoft (MSFT)**, and **Alphabet (GOOGL)** in the near term, even as the TSMC-Nvidia fab deal reinforces that AI infrastructure spending is structural. Apple's decision to run Siri on Gemini and open the door to Claude and ChatGPT is a quiet win for **Alphabet (GOOGL)** and signals that AI model competition will increasingly flow *through* platform gatekeepers. The single biggest near-term market event is the **SpaceX (SPCX) debut on June 12** — watch for a halo effect on aerospace, satellite, and defense tech names. The **Honeywell split on June 29** is a secondary catalyst worth positioning for ahead of the June 15 record date.
