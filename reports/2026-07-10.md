# July 10, 2026 — Tech Deal Debrief

---

## Key Deals & Moves (Last 24 Hours)

- **SK Hynix makes its Nasdaq debut in the largest-ever US listing by a foreign company:** The South Korean memory giant's $26.5 billion ADS offering (177.9M shares at $149) opened at $174.80, up roughly 17%, as investors piled into a pure play on AI memory demand. The blockbuster debut is being watched as a bellwether for the next wave of large tech IPOs later this year.

- **Micron commits $3 billion to GlobalWafers to lock down US silicon wafer supply:** The deal includes up to $500M in strategic financing for GlobalWafers' 300mm wafer fab in Sherman, Texas, plus a 10-year supply agreement guaranteeing Micron priority access to raw silicon for advanced memory — part of Micron's broader push past $250B in total US investment through 2035. Shares rose nearly 7%.

- **French billionaire Xavier Niel takes a 16.2% stake in Vodafone for roughly $6 billion, becoming its largest shareholder:** Niel's Viga bought the stake from UAE's e& at a 13% premium; he says he won't pursue a takeover, but the move signals renewed consolidation interest in European telecom infrastructure. Vodafone's US-listed shares jumped about 13-14% on the news.

- **Circle Internet Group wins final OCC approval to launch a national trust bank ("Circle National Trust"):** The charter lets the USDC issuer bring stablecoin reserve custody under federal regulatory oversight, a milestone win for the crypto-banking push. CRCL shares surged as much as 13-14%.

- **Chip and memory stocks broadly soften ahead of the SK Hynix listing:** Intel slipped nearly 2% and other memory names traded lower in premarket as investors rotated positioning around the new supply of SK Hynix shares hitting the market, even as the semiconductor sector overall remains up sharply for the year on AI infrastructure demand.

---

## Market Synthesis

Today's biggest story is SK Hynix's blowout Nasdaq debut — the largest-ever foreign listing in the US — which alongside Micron's fresh $3B wafer-supply deal with GlobalWafers confirms memory chipmakers are racing to lock in both capital and raw-material supply for a multi-year AI buildout. Watch **SKHY** for how it holds its ~17% pop once the ticker fully converts on July 13, and **MU** for follow-through after its wafer deal. Away from chips, Circle's new bank charter is a real regulatory unlock for the stablecoin sector — worth tracking **CRCL** as USDC's $73B+ in circulation gets a federally regulated custody backstop — while Xavier Niel's opportunistic $6B Vodafone stake is a reminder that telecom infrastructure, the backbone for AI-era data traffic, is drawing fresh strategic capital too. Overall, today's moves reinforce the same theme as this week: money keeps flowing into the physical and financial infrastructure of AI (memory, wafers, custody rails) even as individual chip names like Intel wobble on rotation around the SK Hynix supply.
