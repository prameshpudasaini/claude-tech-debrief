# July 13, 2026 — Tech Deal Debrief

---

## Key Deals & Moves (Last 24 Hours)

- **SK Hynix's blockbuster Nasdaq debut is unraveling fast.** After popping 13% in its ~$28-31B ADR listing last Friday — the largest foreign US listing since SpaceX — SK Hynix shares tumbled roughly 8% in US trading and its Seoul-listed stock sank more than 15% Monday, its worst day on record, as memory-chip euphoria cools. **SKHY/HYMTF** and **MU** are both getting swept up in the reversal.

- **Apple's $30B+ Broadcom chip pact through 2031 is still the week's biggest strategic move.** Apple extended its custom-silicon partnership with Broadcom, committing over $30 billion to fund domestic manufacturing of ASIC chips (including AI-workload silicon) for future Apple products, with Broadcom investing $1.5B to modernize its Fort Collins, Colorado plant. **AVGO** and **AAPL** both rallied on the news.

- **Ondas closed its largest-ever acquisition, buying defense-tech firm DZYNE Technologies for $875.8 million.** The cash-and-stock deal (~$200M cash, ~85M Ondas shares) folds DZYNE's autonomous aircraft and counter-drone tech into Ondas' new "Sentinel" defense division and pushed Ondas to raise its 2026 revenue guidance to $525 million from $390 million. **ONDS** shares have been volatile on dilution concerns.

- **Micron locked in a $22 billion multi-year AI supply deal with Anthropic**, becoming Anthropic's first-choice memory and storage supplier across 16 long-term agreements — the latest sign that AI labs are racing to secure chip supply directly rather than relying on spot markets. Bullish for **MU** longer-term even as the stock rides out this week's memory-sector volatility.

- **SK Hynix and Intel are reportedly testing 2.5D EMIB chip-packaging integration for HBM memory**, while SK Hynix and TSMC separately deepen cooperation on HBM4 base dies and advanced logic — both signs of the AI memory supply chain consolidating around a handful of players. **INTC** and **TSM** both saw reports-driven share moves.

- **The IPO pipeline stays busy: Brookfield-backed data-center operator Csquare is set to price this week aiming to raise up to $1.35 billion**, and small-cap reactor developer Standard Nuclear is pricing alongside it for up to $384 million ahead of a Thursday NYSE listing — both tests of whether investor appetite for AI-infrastructure and energy plays survives the SK Hynix wobble.

- **Anthropic and OpenAI's IPO timelines are diverging.** Anthropic (filed confidentially June 1, last valued near $965B) is still tracking toward a late-2026 debut, while Reuters reports OpenAI (valued at $852B after its $122B round) may push its listing into 2027 given projected 2026 losses near $14 billion — a split that could reshape which AI-labs IPO gets the market's attention first.

- **Wall Street is flagging rising credit risk as tech piles into the bond market to fund AI buildouts.** Record corporate debt issuance tied to data-center and chip capex is drawing caution from analysts, an underlying theme connecting this week's chip deals, memory-stock swings, and IPO activity.

## Market Synthesis

The dominant story this morning is a sharp gut-check on the AI-infrastructure trade: SK Hynix's post-IPO plunge shows investors are starting to question how much further the memory/HBM rally can run, even as the underlying deal flow (Micron-Anthropic, SK Hynix-Intel, SK Hynix-TSMC) keeps confirming that AI chip supply is the industry's central battleground. Apple-Broadcom and Ondas-DZYNE show two different flavors of consolidation — big-cap custom silicon and defense-tech roll-ups — both still finding buyers despite the volatility. Watch **SKHY**, **MU**, **AVGO**, and **AAPL** at the open, and keep an eye on the Csquare and Standard Nuclear IPO pricings later this week as a read on whether risk appetite for AI/infrastructure names has actually cooled or just paused.
