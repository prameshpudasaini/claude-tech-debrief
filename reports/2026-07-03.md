# July 3, 2026 — Tech Deal Debrief

---

## Key Deals & Moves (Last 24–48 Hours)

- **Meta plans to launch a cloud business reselling excess AI compute (July 1–2):** Bloomberg reported Meta is building "Meta Compute," a new unit led by infra chief Santosh Janardhan, Meta Superintelligence Labs' Daniel Gross, and president Dina Powell McCormick, to sell spare AI computing power (and possibly model access) to outside customers — a direct new competitor to AWS, Azure, and Google Cloud. Meta (META) shares popped over 9% on the news, while neocloud rivals CoreWeave (CRWV) and Nebius Group (NBIS) each sank roughly 12%.

- **Micron signs a long-term chip supply deal with General Motors (July 1):** The strategic customer agreement locks in LPDRAM, NOR, and UFS NAND memory supply for GM's next-gen ADAS and in-cabin AI systems, backed by Micron's $2B Manassas, VA fab modernization — a bet on tightening memory supply as AI data centers and automakers compete for the same chips.

- **A sharp chip-sector selloff hit global markets (July 2):** Samsung Electronics fell over 7% and SK Hynix more than 9% in Asia trading; Micron (MU) and SanDisk each dropped over 10% in the US, with Nvidia (NVDA) and Broadcom (AVGO) down 1–2%, as investors grew more skeptical of AI infrastructure spending and pushed a "prove the ROI" narrative on Big Tech.

- **ON Semiconductor agrees to buy Synaptics for nearly $7B in an all-stock deal (late June):** The purchase pushes ON Semi (ON) further into "physical AI" — chips for robotics, industrial automation, and edge AI devices — and remains one of the largest pending semiconductor mergers of the year.

- **Qualcomm agrees to acquire AI infrastructure startup Modular for nearly $4B (June 26):** The deal adds Modular's compiler/software stack to accelerate Qualcomm's (QCOM) push into AI inference chips beyond its core mobile business.

- **Adobe acquires AI image-upscaling startup Topaz Labs:** The deal bolsters Adobe's (ADBE) generative and creative AI tooling, continuing a string of AI bolt-on acquisitions by the company.

- **Together AI raises an $800M Series C led by Aramco Ventures at an $8.3B valuation:** The round underscores continued heavy private investment in AI infrastructure/inference providers even as public AI-adjacent chip stocks wobble.

---

## Market Synthesis

The dominant theme this cycle is a split screen: hyperscalers (Meta, following SpaceX's earlier move) are pivoting to monetize excess AI compute by becoming cloud sellers themselves, squeezing "neocloud" pure-plays like CoreWeave and Nebius, while memory and chip suppliers are whipsawing between long-term supply wins (the Micron-GM deal) and a sentiment-driven selloff as investors question whether AI capex is outrunning near-term returns. Watch **META**, **CRWV**, and **NBIS** for continued divergence if Meta Compute materializes, and watch **MU**, **NVDA**, and **AVGO** for volatility as the "AI bubble" debate plays out against genuinely tightening chip supply. Meanwhile, steady strategic acquisitions (**QCOM**/Modular, **ADBE**/Topaz, **ON**/Synaptics) show buyers still see AI capability gaps worth paying up for, even as public-market enthusiasm cools.
