# June 22, 2026 — Tech Market Debrief

- **Intel stock surges as Apple foundry deal, Google & Nvidia interest crystallize:** Intel shares rallied sharply on June 22 after multiple reports confirmed Apple and Intel have reached a preliminary chip manufacturing agreement using Intel's 18A-P node — Apple's first non-TSMC foundry arrangement for its custom silicon. Reports that Google and Nvidia are also in discussions to use Intel as a backup AI chip manufacturer compounded the move, signaling Intel Foundry may finally be winning credible hyperscaler clients.

- **ITG prices $429M IPO on Nasdaq, targeting AI data center broadband play:** Digital infrastructure contractor ITG — which builds and maintains the physical fiber and broadband networks serving data centers — is seeking to raise up to $429M at a valuation of up to $2.67B, listing on Nasdaq under the ticker ITG. The deal tests whether public market appetite for AI-adjacent infrastructure extends beyond GPU makers to the picks-and-shovels layer of connectivity.

- **Qualcomm in advanced talks to acquire Tenstorrent for $8–10B:** Qualcomm is in advanced negotiations to buy AI chip startup Tenstorrent — founded by chip legend Jim Keller and backed by Jeff Bezos — in a deal valued between $8B and $10B. The acquisition would give QCOM a proprietary RISC-V AI inference architecture and a credible path into the data center chip market to compete with Nvidia and AMD, sending QCOM shares up more than 4%.

- **Broadcom, Apollo & Blackstone close $35B AI compute financing platform for Anthropic:** A special-purpose vehicle co-led by Broadcom, Apollo Global, and Blackstone will deploy $35B to finance more than 1 gigawatt of AI compute infrastructure for Anthropic via the AI XPV Platform. The deal — one of the largest AI infrastructure financing arrangements ever — leases Broadcom XPU capacity to Anthropic through Fluidstack-hosted sites starting mid-2026, with a total platform target exceeding 20 gigawatts through 2028.

- **Intel and UMC sign 10-year advanced packaging partnership in Arizona:** Intel Foundry and Taiwan's UMC formalized a decade-long agreement to co-develop and produce advanced chip packaging in Arizona, positioning the pair to challenge TSMC's packaging dominance as Samsung and TSMC compete intensely for high-volume AI chip clients including Nvidia, AMD, and Tesla.

- **Hg Capital agrees to take OneStream private for $6.4B:** Private equity firm Hg Capital has struck an all-cash deal to acquire OneStream — a publicly traded financial planning and corporate performance management SaaS platform — at $6.4B, taking the company private. The deal underscores continued PE appetite for profitable enterprise software with defensible recurring revenue.

- **Nvidia-Groq inference deal faces ongoing Senate antitrust probe:** The Senate inquiry launched by Senators Warren and Blumenthal into Nvidia's $20B December 2025 licensing-and-acquihire of Groq continued to gain traction, with the FTC also reviewing whether the deal structure — designed to avoid formal merger review — violated antitrust law. Nvidia publicly unveiled the Groq-derived LPX inference rack at GTC 2026, integrating LPU technology into its full-stack AI data center offering.

---

Today's dominant story is Intel's resurgence as a credible AI foundry partner: the Apple deal confirmation and Google/Nvidia backup foundry interest mark a potential inflection point for **INTC**, which has long struggled to win hyperscaler manufacturing commitments. **QCOM** is also a near-term watch given the Tenstorrent talks — a successful deal would reposition Qualcomm as a serious AI data center player beyond mobile. The ITG IPO tests a broadening of AI infrastructure bets from semiconductors into physical connectivity. Broadcom's $35B Anthropic compute vehicle keeps **AVGO** at the center of the AI capex cycle. Across the board, the theme is the same: AI infrastructure spend is cascading further down the stack — from chips to packaging to fiber — and capital is following.
