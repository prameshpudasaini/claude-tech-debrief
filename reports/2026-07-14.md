# July 14, 2026 — Tech Deal Debrief

---

## Key Deals & Moves (Last 24 Hours)

- **Uber is in advanced talks to acquire Delivery Hero, aiming to close a deal as soon as this week.** Bloomberg reports Uber has already built a ~20% stake and is negotiating a takeover that would value the German food-delivery giant well above its recent ~€36 ($41) share price — a marked jump from the €33 offer Delivery Hero rebuffed in May. **UBER** and Delivery Hero (ETR: DHER) are both in play as regulatory approval in overlapping Latin America, Asia, and Europe markets looms as the key hurdle.

- **Microsoft is quietly swapping OpenAI and Anthropic out of its own products in favor of in-house MAI models.** Tens of thousands of weekly AI prompts inside Excel and Outlook are now being served by Microsoft-built models instead of third-party ones, with an exec stating plainly: "We pay a lot of money to Anthropic — so our goal is to reduce and ultimately eliminate that cost." Bullish cost signal for **MSFT** margins, a shot across the bow for **OpenAI** and **Anthropic**'s enterprise-distribution economics.

- **Anthropic is reportedly prepping an IPO filing as early as October, backed by a revenue run-rate near $47 billion and claimed profitability in 2026.** The AI lab's long-term compute deals are said to make its revenue unusually predictable for a startup at this stage, setting up a possible race to market against OpenAI, which Reuters says may now push its own listing into 2027 given projected 2026 losses near $14 billion.

- **Two more AI-adjacent IPOs are set to price this week: data-center operator Csquare and nuclear-fuel maker Standard Nuclear.** Csquare is marketing 50M shares at $23-$27 to raise up to ~$1.25B, while Standard Nuclear is offering 18.25M shares at $18-$21 to raise up to ~$356M — a combined ~$1.6B test of whether investor appetite for AI-infrastructure and energy plays has survived this month's chip-sector wobble.

- **Chip and tech stocks are rebounding today after a cooler-than-expected June CPI print (3.5% YoY vs. 3.8% expected) clawed back some of Monday's AI-valuation-driven selloff.** The VanEck Semiconductor ETF is up nearly 2%, with Applied Materials, Teradyne, and Lam Research all gaining 3-4% and **MU** up more than 2%, following a stretch where Micron, Intel, and AMD had shed $138B+ in value on doubts about AI infrastructure ROI, Meta's capacity-reselling "Meta Compute" plan, and reports of slowing SK Hynix HBM expansion.

---

## Market Synthesis

Today's headline is consolidation in food delivery (Uber-Delivery Hero) layered on top of a deepening AI-labs arms race: Microsoft is moving to cut its own OpenAI/Anthropic spend just as Anthropic signals it's IPO-ready with real profitability, a combination that could reshape both enterprise AI pricing power and which AI lab captures investor attention first when it lists. Meanwhile the Csquare and Standard Nuclear IPOs are a live gauge of whether the "AI infrastructure trade" — badly bruised by this month's chip selloff and SK Hynix's post-IPO stumble — still has buyers at scale. Watch **UBER**, **MSFT**, and **MU** at the open, and keep an eye on where Csquare and Standard Nuclear price relative to their ranges later this week as the next real-time read on risk appetite for AI/infrastructure names.
