# June 24, 2026 — Tech Market Debrief

- **Google, Meta, Unity, and Moloco each take minority stakes in AppsFlyer at $2.7B valuation:** The four advertising platforms jointly led a $1B+ Series E in AppsFlyer, an Israeli mobile measurement and attribution company, marking a rare co-investment by direct competitors. The deal signals how critical independent measurement infrastructure has become as AI reshapes digital advertising, and gives each platform a seat at the table of the attribution layer that governs how their own ad dollars get credited.

- **Zeta Global surges 6% on Palantir Foundry partnership:** Zeta Global (ZETA) announced it will rebuild its entire Data Cloud on Palantir's Foundry platform, creating a unified AI infrastructure linking operational data to marketing execution at enterprise scale. CEO David Steinberg said the deal could generate more than $100M in annual revenue for Zeta in coming years — the stock's pop reflects the market pricing in a meaningful re-acceleration of Zeta's AI-driven growth.

- **Micron signs multi-year AI infrastructure supply and investment deal with Anthropic:** Micron Technology formalized a strategic partnership with Anthropic that includes a multi-year agreement to supply memory and storage products for Anthropic's AI systems alongside a direct investment in the AI lab. The deal positions **MU** as a preferred memory partner for one of the world's most valuable AI companies, adding a durable supply-chain anchor as Anthropic continues its explosive growth toward a reported $1T valuation.

- **FuelCell Energy lands 380 MW data center power agreement with Fit Energy:** FuelCell Energy (FCEL) announced a strategic agreement with Fit Energy USA LP to supply up to 380 MW of clean, baseload on-site power to data centers using utility-scale fuel cell technology, with an initial 30 MW tranche beginning delivery later this year. The deal is one of the largest clean-energy commitments to the data center sector announced this year and pushes FCEL squarely into the AI infrastructure power narrative.

- **SoFi Technologies acquires Composer Securities, an AI-powered automated trading platform:** SoFi finalized its acquisition of Composer Securities, which lets retail investors build and deploy automated trading strategies using AI. While the deal terms were not disclosed and SoFi treated it as non-material, the acquisition deepens SoFi's push toward AI-native financial products as it competes with both traditional brokerages and fintech rivals.

- **SpaceX's $60B Cursor acquisition continues reshaping the enterprise software landscape:** The ripple effects of SpaceX's all-stock acquisition of Anysphere — maker of the Cursor AI coding tool — announced June 16 continue to move markets, as investors re-price AI-native developer tools as a category. The deal, the largest VC-backed startup acquisition in history, signals that post-IPO SpaceX is moving aggressively into enterprise software using its freshly liquid public stock.

- **Trump Media (DJT) and TAE Technologies merger inches toward Q4 2026 close as Truth Social spin-off talks emerge:** The $6B merger between Trump Media & Technology Group and nuclear fusion startup TAE Technologies is targeting a Q4 2026 close, with both parties confirming progress. A new wrinkle: TMTG, TAE, and Texas Ventures Acquisition III are in discussions to spin off Truth Social into a separate public company post-close, which could simplify the combined entity's story as a pure-play fusion energy stock.

---

The clearest theme across today's deals is AI infrastructure consolidation at every layer of the stack — memory (Micron-Anthropic), data platforms (Zeta-Palantir), clean energy for compute (FuelCell Energy), and ad measurement (AppsFlyer). **MU** and **ZETA** are the most direct near-term trading ideas from today's news; **FCEL** is a watch for investors playing the AI power buildout beyond the usual hyperscaler names. The AppsFlyer co-investment by Google and Meta is unusual enough to deserve attention — it suggests the biggest ad platforms see independent attribution as a shared utility worth protecting rather than a competitive threat to squash. Meanwhile, the SpaceX-Cursor overhang keeps pressure on publicly traded developer-tool companies like **GTLB** and **DDOG** as the market recalibrates what AI-native software is worth.
