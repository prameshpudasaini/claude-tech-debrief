# 2026-06-30 — Tech Deals & Market Movers

---

- **Rocket Lab to acquire Iridium Communications for $8B (June 29):** Rocket Lab struck a cash-and-stock deal to buy satellite-communications provider Iridium, paying $27 cash plus stock per share (~$54/share total). The combo pairs Rocket Lab's launch vehicles with Iridium's global L-band network, licensed spectrum, and 2.5M+ subscribers — a direct play to replicate SpaceX's Starlink model. RKLB jumped ~12-16%, IRDM soared ~22-25% on the news; Rocket Lab lined up a $3.6B bridge loan from Deutsche Bank and Wells Fargo to fund it.

- **Alphabet joins the Dow Jones Industrial Average, replacing Verizon (June 29):** GOOGL shares rose 3.7-4.8% as Alphabet began trading as a Dow component, becoming the fifth Magnificent Seven member in the index alongside Nvidia, Amazon, Apple, and Microsoft. The swap tilts the Dow further toward AI, cloud, and digital-ad exposure and triggers index-fund rebalancing flows.

- **Onsemi (ON Semiconductor) to acquire Synaptics for ~$7B in an all-stock deal (this week):** The largest acquisition in onsemi's history expands its reach into edge AI, connected devices, robotics, automotive, and industrial hardware, adding roughly $30B to its addressable market.

- **Adobe acquires Topaz Labs (June 25):** Adobe bought AI image/video upscaling and enhancement startup Topaz Labs to bolster its generative and creative-AI tooling, deepening the AI feature race against Canva and emerging AI-native creative tools.

- **Qualcomm's ~$4B Modular acquisition continues moving through deal paperwork:** Qualcomm's all-stock purchase of AI software startup Modular (maker of the Mojo language and MAX AI platform) — aimed at making chips from Nvidia, AMD, and Intel interchangeable for AI workloads — remains a live story as SEC filings progress; still a watch item for QCOM vs. NVDA's CUDA moat.

- **Market backdrop:** The Dow closed above 52,000 for the first time and the Nasdaq jumped 2.1% Monday as US-Iran tensions eased, capping the S&P 500's best quarter since 2020 — a risk-on tailwind that's amplifying reaction to the week's deal news.

---

## What It Means

Two consolidation themes are converging this week: legacy hardware/communications players racing to bundle infrastructure the way SpaceX did with Starlink (Rocket Lab-Iridium), and chip/software companies buying AI capability outright rather than building it (onsemi-Synaptics, Qualcomm-Modular, Adobe-Topaz). Watch **RKLB**, **IRDM**, **ON**, **SYNA**, and **QCOM** for continued post-deal volatility, plus **GOOGL** on Dow-rebalancing flows. With the broader market hitting record highs into quarter-end, expect dealmaking pace to stay elevated as companies look to lock in strategic AI and infrastructure positions before any pullback.
