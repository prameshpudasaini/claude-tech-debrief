# Tech Deal Debrief — June 4, 2026

## Key Deals & Announcements

- **Quantinuum (QNT) begins trading on Nasdaq today at $60/share** — Honeywell's quantum computing spinoff priced its IPO above the $53–55 target range, raising ~$1.68B in an upsized offering of 28 million shares; this is the first major pure-play quantum computing company to list on U.S. exchanges at scale, with underwriters J.P. Morgan and Morgan Stanley holding a 30-day greenshoe option on an additional 4.2 million shares.

- **SpaceX IPO pricing in 7 days, trading June 12 on Nasdaq as SPCX** — SpaceX set a $135/share roadshow price targeting a $1.77 trillion valuation and a ~$75 billion raise, which would shatter the all-time IPO record (Saudi Aramco raised $29.4B in 2019) by more than 2.5x; Elon Musk will retain over 82% voting control post-offering, and Goldman Sachs leads the underwriting syndicate.

- **Nvidia strikes $20B licensing deal with Groq for LPU inference technology** — Nvidia inked a non-exclusive license for Groq's Language Processing Unit architecture, which uses on-chip SRAM to cut inference latency dramatically; Groq's founder and CEO Jonathan Ross moves to Nvidia, while Groq continues operating independently under a new CEO — a signal that Nvidia is hedging its GPU dominance by acquiring specialized inference IP.

- **IonQ acquiring SkyWater Technology for $1.8B in cash and stock** — the deal ($15 cash + $20 in IonQ shares per SkyWater share) creates the only vertically integrated full-stack quantum computing company with domestic chip fabrication; IonQ says securing SkyWater's Minnesota fab will accelerate its 2-million-qubit architecture by up to a year, with close expected in Q2 or Q3 2026.

- **SoftBank acquires DigitalBridge for ~$4B** — the deal, expected to close in H1 2026, hands SoftBank direct ownership of a digital infrastructure investment platform, complementing its massive AI data center bets with Arm, OpenAI, and the AI Infrastructure Partnership; deepens SoftBank's vertical integration across AI chips, software, and physical infrastructure.

- **Lumen Technologies acquires multi-cloud networking firm Alkira for $475M** — Lumen is buying Alkira to build out programmable, AI-optimized networking fabric; the deal targets the bottleneck between AI compute and enterprise connectivity as agentic AI workloads drive surging bandwidth demand.

- **Health Catalyst divests Vitalware to Med-Metrix for $147M** (signed June 4) — the healthcare analytics firm is shedding its charge-description-master unit and using the full proceeds to retire its ~$160M senior secured term loan, simplifying its capital structure and refocusing on its core data operating platform.

## Synthesis

Today's marquee event is Quantinuum's Nasdaq debut, opening a new chapter for quantum computing as an investable public-market category — watch for IonQ (IONQ) to trade on the Quantinuum pricing signal, since its SkyWater acquisition makes it the most direct pure-play comp. The bigger story one week out is SpaceX: at $75B, the June 12 listing will absorb a historically large share of institutional IPO allocation, and its gravitational pull may compress near-term appetite for smaller growth names. Meanwhile, Nvidia's Groq deal and IonQ's SkyWater acquisition both point to vertical integration as the defining M&A theme of 2026 — companies are buying their supply chains, not just their competitors. Key stocks to watch: **NVDA** (Groq deal and inference strategy), **HON** (Quantinuum parent repricing), **IONQ** (SkyWater close and QNT comp), **LUMN** (Alkira AI networking pivot), and **SOFTB** (DigitalBridge close).
