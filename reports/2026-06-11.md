# June 11, 2026 — Tech Market Debrief

## Major Deals & Moves (Past 24 Hours)

- **Oracle's earnings beat triggers a double-digit selloff (June 10–11):** Oracle reported Q4 FY2026 revenue of $19.18B and non-GAAP EPS of $2.11, both above estimates, with OCI cloud infrastructure growth above 40% and RPO up 28%. Despite the beat, shares plunged more than 10% (from ~$248 to ~$184) as investors fixated on negative free cash flow of -$23.7B for the year, falling return on invested capital, and plans to raise significant new debt and equity in FY2027 to keep funding its AI data center buildout.

- **AI semiconductor stocks slide broadly as Nasdaq drops 2% (June 10–11):** The Nasdaq Composite fell about 509 points to 25,169.50, with **Broadcom (AVGO)** down 5.1% and **Nvidia (NVDA)** down 3.7%. The drop reflects profit-taking in richly valued AI chip names, a hot 4.2% CPI print reviving rate-hike fears, escalating US-Iran tensions, and capital rotating out of chip stocks ahead of the SpaceX IPO.

- **SpaceX IPO is multiple times oversubscribed ahead of Friday's debut:** Bloomberg reports SpaceX's record-setting $75B offering — priced at $135/share for a $1.77T valuation, ticker **SPCX** — is oversubscribed several times over heading into its June 12 Nasdaq listing. It would be the largest IPO in history, vaulting SpaceX above Tesla as roughly the 7th most valuable US public company.

- **Nvidia and SK hynix announce multiyear AI memory partnership:** The companies will co-develop next-generation memory for Nvidia's upcoming Vera Rubin AI supercomputers, Vera CPUs, RTX Spark PCs, and Jetson Thor robotics platforms, deepening Nvidia's control over its AI hardware supply chain as its next chip generation ramps.

- **TSMC and Nvidia expand AI manufacturing partnership:** TSMC will apply Nvidia's AI and accelerated-computing tools across its fabs for lithography, defect detection, and production optimization — a deal that could lift TSMC's yields while reinforcing Nvidia's reach into the chipmaking process itself.

- **IonQ's $1.8B acquisition of SkyWater Technology advances toward close:** IonQ's cash-and-stock deal for chipmaker SkyWater — creating the first vertically integrated quantum hardware platform — remains on track to close in Q2/Q3 2026 after shareholder approval, and pulls forward IonQ's 200,000-qubit chip testing roadmap to 2028.

---

## Market Synthesis

The AI trade hit a real stress test today: Oracle beat on earnings but got punished for its cash-burn and financing plans, spreading "show me the returns" anxiety from chips into cloud and software. Combined with hot inflation data, Middle East tensions, and capital being pulled toward tomorrow's record SpaceX IPO, that drove a broad AI-stock pullback led by **AVGO**, **NVDA**, and **ORCL** — all worth watching for continued volatility, along with **SPCX** once it lists Friday to see how much liquidity the listing absorbs from the sector. At the same time, corporate-level AI infrastructure dealmaking (Nvidia-SK hynix, TSMC-Nvidia, IonQ-SkyWater) shows no signs of slowing, suggesting today's weakness is more about valuation and financing jitters than an actual slowdown in AI investment.
