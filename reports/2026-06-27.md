# 2026-06-27 — Tech Deals & Market Movers

---

- **Onsemi acquires Synaptics for ~$7B (all-stock):** ON Semiconductor agreed to buy Synaptics in its largest-ever acquisition, offering Synaptics shareholders 1.35 Onsemi shares per share — a 19% premium. The deal targets edge AI and "physical AI" (AI embedded in devices, robots, and industrial hardware), expanding Onsemi's addressable market by $30B to $243B by 2030.

- **Qualcomm (QCOM) falls 5.12% June 26 on Modular digestion:** One day after formally filing its $3.92B all-stock acquisition of AI software startup Modular, QCOM slid sharply as investors processed share dilution risk. Modular's MAX platform runs AI across Nvidia, AMD, and Intel chips without code rewrites — a direct challenge to CUDA — but markets are pricing in execution risk.

- **PHLX chip index down 5.3% Friday, 7.9% for the week:** A surge in memory costs hit device makers broadly, with Apple among those affected. The sell-off triggered $20B in tech fund outflows — the steepest weekly chip drawdown of 2026 and a potential reset point for AI infrastructure sentiment.

- **SpaceX (SPCX) at $151 in premarket, well off $202 post-IPO high:** Shares fell 1.27% in premarket June 27 as the $60B Cursor (Anysphere) deal awaits its expected Q3 close. With SpaceX now also running a $6.3B compute agreement with Reflection AI, the stock remains the most volatility-rich name in the market — up 16% on the Cursor announcement, now retracing.

- **OpenAI IPO delay reports drag Nasdaq June 26:** Reports circulated that OpenAI's public offering timeline has slipped, contributing to broader tech softness. OpenAI filed confidentially in early June targeting an $850B+ valuation, but the company has signaled no firm date, and questions about its data center spending commitments ($600B through 2030) are resurfacing.

- **Anthropic's ~$965B confidential IPO filing advances:** Filed June 1 after a $65B Series H round, Anthropic is now the next AI mega-listing on deck. The company says it is near its first quarterly profit — a key differentiator from OpenAI heading into public markets.

- **Paramount/Warner Bros. Discovery merger on track for Q3 close:** Shareholder approval came April 23; regulatory clearances remain pending. Once closed, the combined Paramount-WBD entity becomes a major streaming rival to Netflix and reshapes the media-tech competitive map heading into 2027.

## What It Means

The dominant theme this week is a reality check on AI hardware and infrastructure: the PHLX chip index's near-8% weekly decline signals that markets are beginning to question the pace of AI capex spending as memory costs rise and near-term returns stay murky — watch **NVDA**, **AMD**, and **QCOM** for stabilization signals. Chipmakers are responding by buying their way into software: Qualcomm's Modular deal and Onsemi's Synaptics acquisition both point to a strategy shift from silicon leadership toward full-stack AI solutions, which could revalue these names if execution holds. Meanwhile, the AI IPO pipeline is building toward a historic fall window — **Anthropic** near profitability, **OpenAI** filing but stalling — with **SPCX** as the live proxy trade for AI software platform bets. The Paramount-WBD close approaching means the streaming consolidation trade is nearly complete; investors should watch how that combined entity positions against Netflix once the deal is done.
