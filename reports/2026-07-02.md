# July 2, 2026 — Tech Deal Debrief

---

## Key Deals & Moves (Last 24–48 Hours)

- **Bending Spoons IPOs on Nasdaq at an $18.4B valuation (July 1):** The Italian "digital roll-up" firm behind AOL, Vimeo, Evernote, WeTransfer, and Eventbrite priced its offering at $29/share (above the $26–$28 range), raising $1.68B — up sharply from its $11B private valuation in 2025. Shares (ticker: BSP) closed up nearly 40% on debut, one of the strongest tech IPO pops of the year.

- **National Grid invests $1.75B for a 35% stake in Joulent, tied to a Microsoft data-center power deal (July 1–2):** The UK utility is backing Joulent's "Project Kilby," a 2.67-gigawatt co-located power facility in West Texas (a 50/50 build with Chevron) that will feed a Microsoft-operated AI data center under a 20-year power-purchase agreement. It's the latest sign that Big Tech is locking up dedicated power supply outside the traditional grid to feed AI compute buildouts.

- **Brookfield quintuples its Bloom Energy AI-power financing to $25B (June 30):** Brookfield expanded its funding framework for Bloom's fuel-cell-based data-center power systems from $5B to $25B as AI developers race for faster alternatives to grid interconnection. Bloom Energy (BE) shares jumped about 12% on the news.

- **Lime goes public on Nasdaq (July 1):** The micromobility company (scooters/e-bikes) raised $167M in its long-awaited IPO, capping years of speculation about a public listing and adding another name to 2026's reopened tech IPO window.

- **Semiconductor stocks sell off despite a risk-on tape (July 2):** The Dow, S&P 500, and Nasdaq all rose after a soft June jobs report (57K vs. 110K expected) boosted Fed rate-cut hopes, but chip stocks broke from the rally — the iShares Semiconductor ETF (SOXX) fell over 3% as investors took profits after chipmakers' 80%+ first-half surge. AMD and other AI chip names slipped even as the broader market climbed.

---

## Market Synthesis

The dominant theme this cycle is AI infrastructure power, not chips: National Grid, Brookfield, and Bloom Energy are all racing to lock up dedicated electricity supply for data centers, with Microsoft directly underwriting the Joulent project via a 20-year PPA. At the same time, the IPO window is wide open and rewarding new listings generously — Bending Spoons' 40% pop and Lime's successful debut suggest investors have fresh risk appetite for tech names outside the mega-cap AI trade. The one sour note is semiconductors, which broke from Thursday's broad rally on profit-taking after a blistering first-half run — a reminder that chip valuations may be getting stretched even as AI infrastructure spending keeps accelerating elsewhere. Stocks to watch: **BSP**, **BE**, **MSFT**, **AMD**, and **SOXX** for continued divergence between AI power/infrastructure plays and chip names.
