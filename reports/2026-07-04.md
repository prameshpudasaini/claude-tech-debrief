# July 4, 2026 — Tech Deal Debrief

---

## Key Deals & Moves (Last 24–48 Hours)

- **Google loses its final EU antitrust appeal over the €4.1B Android fine (July 2):** The EU's top court, the Court of Justice, upheld the record penalty for Google abusing Android's dominance to favor its own apps. Alphabet (GOOGL) has no further appeal option — the ruling is final and adds to Brussels' growing pile of enforcement wins against Big Tech, keeping regulatory overhang on Alphabet shares.

- **Unitree Robotics wins approval for a ~$620M Shanghai STAR Market IPO (July 3):** China's securities regulator cleared the humanoid/quadruped robot maker's listing in just 104 days — the fastest review under STAR Market rules — implying a valuation near $5.9B. More than 40 Chinese robotics stocks hit daily limit-up on the news, a read-through worth watching for US-listed humanoid and industrial-automation names.

- **Palantir upgraded to Buy by D.A. Davidson (this week):** Shares rose about 4% as the firm cited Palantir's (PLTR) competitive positioning in AI software and defense contracts and called the valuation attractive relative to growth — a sentiment data point for the broader AI-software trade.

- **Salesforce's ~$3.6B acquisition of AI agent startup Fin moves toward close:** Fin's AI agent handles end-to-end customer service across chat, email, phone, and Slack; folding it in deepens Salesforce's (CRM) Agentforce push as AI-native customer-service tools become a competitive battleground.

- **Fallout continues from Meta's "Meta Compute" cloud pivot (late last week):** Wall Street commentary (CNBC) is now digesting the margin implications of Meta (META) becoming a compute reseller — good for top-line optionality on its $115–135B 2026 capex, but a drag on blended margins — while neocloud names CoreWeave (CRWV) and Nebius (NBIS) remain pressured on the competitive threat.

- **Qualcomm's $3.9B Modular deal and ON Semiconductor's $7B Synaptics buy remain the week's largest pending semiconductor M&A**, both still awaiting close and continuing to anchor the "AI infrastructure bolt-on" narrative in chips.

## Market Synthesis

With US markets closed for the July 4th holiday, the past day's action is more about regulatory and sentiment signals than new mega-deals: Alphabet takes a final, if largely priced-in, antitrust hit in Europe, while a fast-tracked Chinese robotics IPO (Unitree) and a fresh analyst upgrade on Palantir both point to continued investor appetite for AI-adjacent growth stories even as chip stocks wobble. The bigger through-line from the past week is still Meta's move into compute-reselling, which is reshaping how the Street values hyperscalers versus neocloud pure-plays (**META** up, **CRWV**/**NBIS** down) — expect that debate, plus pending closes on **QCOM**/Modular and **ON**/Synaptics, to dominate again once trading resumes Monday. Watch **GOOGL** for any follow-through on the EU ruling and **PLTR** for continued momentum into next week.
