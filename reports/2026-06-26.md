# 2026-06-26 — Tech Deals & Market Movers

---

- **Apple + Intel announce U.S. chip manufacturing alliance:** Apple agreed to partner with Intel to co-design and manufacture certain chips domestically, with the deal initially targeting lower-volume and mature-node processors before potentially expanding. Intel shares surged sharply on the news — the partnership bolsters Intel's foundry business and gives Apple a hedge against single-supplier risk on silicon.

- **Elroy Air going public via SPAC at $800M valuation (today):** Autonomous heavy-cargo drone maker Elroy Air announced a business combination with Columbus Circle Capital Corp II (Nasdaq: CMII), targeting a Q4 2026 close. The deal includes Kratos Defense & Security Solutions as exclusive U.S. manufacturer and a $200M joint venture with Barq Group to build a facility in Abu Dhabi serving the MENA region.

- **EigenQ quantum security company set to list at ~$3B via SPAC:** Quantum tech firm EigenQ agreed to go public by merging with Silicon Valley Acquisition Corp (Nasdaq: SVAQ), targeting a Nasdaq listing under ticker EIGQ. EigenQ focuses on post-quantum cybersecurity, communications, and sensing for enterprise and government customers — one of the first pure-play quantum security names headed to public markets.

- **AI startup funding wave on June 25 — infrastructure and healthcare lead:** European healthtech AI company Alan raised €480M in a Series G; Trase pulled in $107M seed funding (backed by ARCH Venture Partners) for AI that automates clinical paperwork at health systems; Scaled Cognition raised $100M Series A from Khosla Ventures for AI reliability in enterprise workflows; Runpod raised $100M for GPU cloud infrastructure; and Sail Research raised $80M for long-horizon AI agent infrastructure at a $450M valuation.

- **OpenAI locks in multibillion-dollar compute deals beyond Microsoft:** OpenAI announced multi-billion-dollar agreements with Nvidia, Broadcom, Oracle, Amazon, and Google — a deliberate move to reduce its dependence on a single cloud and hardware vendor. The deals reinforce Nvidia and Broadcom's positions as mission-critical suppliers while adding pressure to Microsoft's exclusive-partner narrative.

- **POET Technologies + Lumilens partner on AI optical networking:** POET Technologies and Lumilens announced a deal to co-develop wafer-level photonic integration targeting AI optical network infrastructure. The partnership represents a push to move AI data-center interconnects from copper to photonics, a transition investors in optical networking names have been watching.

- **Applied Materials + TSMC launch joint R&D at new Silicon Valley center:** Applied Materials and TSMC announced a research collaboration at Applied's new Silicon Valley R&D center, targeting advanced materials engineering and process integration technologies. The partnership strengthens both companies' competitive positioning as AI chip complexity drives demand for next-node process innovation.

---

## What It Means

Today's activity highlights two converging forces: the ongoing AI infrastructure arms race and a resurgence of SPAC and public-market activity in defense-tech and emerging-tech categories. The Apple-Intel chip partnership is the clearest market signal — watch **INTC** for a sustained re-rating if the alliance confirms a real foundry revenue stream, and **AAPL** for supply-chain resilience reads. The flood of AI infrastructure funding (Runpod, Sail Research, Scaled Cognition) reflects institutional conviction that AI compute and reliability tooling still have years of buildout ahead — a tailwind for **NVDA**, **AMD**, and data-center REITs. The Elroy Air and EigenQ SPAC deals signal that the blank-check market is reopening for defense-adjacent and deep-tech names, with quantum security becoming a category worth watching as post-quantum standards roll out. OpenAI's deliberate broadening beyond Microsoft puts **MSFT**'s AI-moat thesis under mild pressure while lifting **ORCL**, **AVGO**, and **AMZN** as compute beneficiaries.
