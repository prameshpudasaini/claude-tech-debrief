# July 7, 2026 — Tech Deal Debrief

---

## Key Deals & Moves (Last 24 Hours)

- **Google backs Proxima Fusion in a €411 million ($468M) round, valuing the fusion startup at €2.4 billion:** Alphabet and German utility RWE co-anchored the financing, the largest private fusion-energy raise in Europe, which will fund Proxima's "Alpha" stellarator demonstrator near Munich. It's the latest sign hyperscalers are investing directly in next-gen power generation to meet AI data-center electricity demand.

- **Brookfield-backed data center operator Csquare sets IPO terms, targeting a $4.18B valuation:** Csquare plans to sell 50 million shares at $23–$27 to raise up to $1.35 billion, listing on the NYSE under "CSQR." The Dallas-based company operates 64 data center sites across the US, Canada, and UK and is riding the same AI-infrastructure demand wave that drove recent data-center IPOs.

- **SpaceX officially joins the Nasdaq-100 index today**, the first company to enter under the exchange's new "fast-track" rule for mega-IPOs. The addition forces index funds tracking the Nasdaq-100 to buy SPCX shares, adding a mechanical source of demand on top of the stock's post-IPO momentum.

- **Chip stocks extend Monday's rout into Tuesday's premarket:** Micron (-5.6% premarket after Monday's 13% plunge) and Nvidia (-2.1%) led continued semiconductor weakness, alongside declines in Intel and AMD. The selloff stems from reports that SK Hynix is slowing HBM memory expansion, mounting investor doubts about near-term returns on AI infrastructure spending, and a more hawkish Fed under new Chair Kevin Warsh.

- **Specialty chemical maker Solstice to acquire Element Solutions for $14.5 billion in cash and stock:** The deal broadens Solstice's exposure to the AI infrastructure supply chain — semiconductor fabrication materials, advanced chip packaging, and thermal management — but Solstice shares plunged 15% on investor concern about price and integration risk, with the CEO publicly defending the rationale.

---

## Market Synthesis

Today's moves split into two camps: durable, long-horizon AI infrastructure bets (Google funding fusion power, Brookfield spinning off a data-center REIT-like IPO) versus a market that's suddenly nervous about near-term AI capex payoff, as seen in the extending chip selloff and the rough reception for Solstice's supply-chain acquisition. Watch **MU**, **NVDA**, **INTC**, and **AMD** for whether the semiconductor rout stabilizes or deepens, plus **CSQR** when it prices, as a read on whether IPO investors still want direct AI-infrastructure exposure despite the chip-sector jitters. The Solstice-Element Solutions reaction (-15%) is a signal worth tracking: with valuations stretched after 2026's AI-driven rally, the market is punishing "story" acquisitions more than it did earlier this year, which could cool further M&A pace in the space.
