# 2026-07-15

- **ASML raises 2026 sales outlook on AI chip demand.** Shares jumped ~3.6% premarket ahead of Wednesday's earnings, with the company citing stronger-than-expected AI-driven lithography orders — a bellwether signal ahead of TSMC's earnings Thursday.
- **Broadcom and Apple extend their chip supply partnership through 2031.** The news helped lift the Nasdaq-100 roughly 1.3% and pushed Broadcom shares up over 9%, building on Apple's earlier $30 billion commitment to Broadcom for U.S.-based chipmaking.
- **Alibaba wins Chinese regulatory approval to run its Qwen AI models on Apple devices sold in China.** The deal deepens Apple's AI partner stack in its largest overseas market and pulls Alibaba further into the smartphone AI race.
- **Mitsubishi Heavy Industries partners with Nvidia on AI data-center infrastructure.** The collaboration covers cooling systems and energy-management equipment for AI data centers; MHI shares rose nearly 5% on the announcement.
- **CleanSpark signs a 20-year, $6.6 billion data-center lease in Georgia.** The deal shifts the bitcoin miner's revenue mix toward steady, high-margin AI-infrastructure income and reduces its exposure to crypto price swings; shares rose 8.8% on the news.
- **Anthropic opens early talks with Samsung to co-develop a custom AI chip.** The move follows Google, Amazon, Meta, and OpenAI in building in-house silicon to reduce reliance on Nvidia GPUs, and comes on the heels of Anthropic's recent multiyear memory-supply tie-up with Micron.

Today's flow is all about AI infrastructure lock-in rather than classic tech M&A: chipmakers and hyperscalers are cementing multi-year supply and capacity deals (Broadcom-Apple, MHI-Nvidia, CleanSpark) instead of one-off buyouts, while ASML's upgraded outlook and TSMC's earnings tomorrow will be the real test of whether the AI capex story still has legs. Watch ASML, TSMC, and Broadcom into earnings, plus Nvidia-adjacent suppliers, for the next directional move in semis. The Anthropic-Samsung talks are also a signal worth tracking: as more AI labs pursue custom chips, Nvidia's near-term pricing power faces a slow-building competitive threat even as overall AI spending keeps expanding.
