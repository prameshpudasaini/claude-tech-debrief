# 2026-07-18

- **Stripe + Advent International bid $53B for PayPal.** Stripe teamed up with private equity firm Advent International on a joint takeover offer for PayPal at $60.50/share (a 28% premium), backed by ~$50B in committed bank financing. PYPL shares jumped on the news; the two firms would split ownership equally with no plan to break up the company.
- **Meta in talks to lease Anthropic $10B of AI compute.** Meta and Anthropic are negotiating a deal (pitched by Anthropic in June) where Meta would rent out AI data-center capacity to Anthropic for up to $10B over two years, paid monthly. It would be an unusual arrangement — Meta competes with Claude via Llama but would also become its infrastructure landlord — and signals how severe the AI compute shortage has become.
- **Apple gets China approval for Apple Intelligence via Alibaba's Qwen.** China's internet regulator cleared Apple Intelligence for iOS/iPadOS/macOS/visionOS after Apple agreed to integrate Alibaba's Qwen model (Baidu is also involved) instead of building its own China-compliant AI. Alibaba and Baidu shares jumped in Hong Kong on the news; Apple did $20.5B in Greater China sales last quarter.
- **Micron signs AI-auto chip supply deals with Qualcomm and Harman.** Micron locked in long-term agreements to supply memory and storage components for AI-enabled vehicles, tying its automotive memory roadmap to Qualcomm's compute platforms.
- **Kioxia ordered to pay Viasat $229M for patent infringement.** A federal jury in Texas found the Japanese memory chipmaker infringed a Viasat patent related to computer memory technology; Kioxia shares plunged more than 14% on the ruling.
- **Fireworks AI raises $1.5B Series D at a $17.5B valuation, with Nvidia participating.** The AI inference/model-serving startup's round was led by Atreides, Index Ventures and TCV, with Nvidia among the returning investors — another data point on Nvidia's strategy of taking stakes across the AI infrastructure stack it also sells into.
- **Chip stocks sold off broadly this week on AI-valuation jitters.** The VanEck Semiconductor ETF (SMH) dropped nearly 9% over the week — its third weekly decline in four — after Chinese startup Moonshot AI unveiled a new model it claims narrows the gap with top US models, reviving fears that Chinese competition could pressure the economics behind the US AI buildout.

The dominant theme is the AI compute crunch reshaping who owns and rents infrastructure: Meta weighing whether to become an compute landlord for a rival lab (Anthropic), and Nvidia continuing to invest directly into the startups that consume its chips (Fireworks). Watch PYPL and financial-services names for M&A premium re-ratings after the Stripe/Advent bid, and keep an eye on semiconductor names (SMH, MU, KIOXIA, NVDA) — the sector is jumpy on any sign that cheaper Chinese AI models could dent US AI capex assumptions. Apple's China AI approval, via BABA's Qwen, is a genuine near-term catalyst for both AAPL's stalled China growth story and Alibaba's AI monetization narrative.
