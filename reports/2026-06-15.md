# June 15, 2026 — Tech Market Debrief

## Major Deals & Moves (Past 24 Hours)

- **Fox Corporation agrees to buy Roku for $22B, creating a streaming and live-TV giant:** Fox will pay $160/share (a ~20% premium to Thursday's close) in a cash-and-stock deal — $96 cash plus 0.9693 Fox Class A shares per Roku share — funded partly with $8B in new debt. Roku surged ~17.5% to $140.64 on the news, while FOX shares dropped about 8% as investors weighed the dilution and debt load; Roku CEO Anthony Wood will join the combined company's board, with close expected in H1 2027.

- **U.S.-Iran peace framework sparks a broad tech rally:** A weekend announcement of a tentative deal to end the U.S.-Iran conflict and reopen the Strait of Hormuz sent the Nasdaq up roughly 2% and the Technology sector (XLK) up over 3%, while oil prices eased toward the low $80s — a major risk-on tailwind for AI and growth stocks heading into the week.

- **SpaceX (SPCX) extends its post-IPO surge toward a $2T+ valuation:** Riding the broader risk-on wave, SpaceX shares climbed another ~6% Monday, building on last week's 19% debut pop and continuing to anchor sentiment around the AI/space mega-IPO trade.

- **SoftBank leads a 12%+ Asian tech rally on the peace-deal news:** SoftBank Group (SFTBY) jumped more than 12% as Asian tech and chip-adjacent names rallied alongside the broader risk-on move — a read-through for U.S.-listed semiconductor and AI names at the open.

- **Google commits $1.5B to expand its AI data center campus in Jackson County, Alabama:** Alphabet (GOOGL) said the investment, spanning 2026-2027, will cover the site's own power needs and associated infrastructure costs — another data point in hyperscalers' accelerating AI capex race.

- **Micron (MU) jumps as TD Cowen more than doubles its price target to $1,500:** The upgrade cites surging DRAM demand and a 2027 EPS projection of $150, adding to a string of bullish memory-chip calls as AI infrastructure spending keeps pushing memory prices higher.

- **Chip stocks broadly firm ahead of Marvell's June 22 S&P 500 debut:** Taiwan Semiconductor (TSM) rallied about 4% and Micron gained sharply in premarket trading, with semiconductor strength continuing to be a primary pillar of the indices as Marvell (MRVL) — up nearly 240% YTD — and Flex prepare to join the S&P 500 next week.

---

## Market Synthesis

The dominant theme today is **risk-on across tech**, driven by the U.S.-Iran peace framework, which lifted the Nasdaq ~2%, sent SoftBank up 12%+, and extended SpaceX's post-IPO run toward a $2T valuation — a macro tailwind that should support AI and growth names broadly at the open. Within that backdrop, the **Fox-Roku $22B deal** is the standout strategic move: it reshapes the streaming/connected-TV landscape (watch **ROKU** for continued strength and **FOX** for dilution-driven weakness) and signals more traditional media consolidation as legacy players race to build first-party CTV data and ad scale. On the AI-infrastructure side, **Google's $1.5B Alabama data center commitment** and the continued memory-chip bull case (TD Cowen's $1,500 Micron target, **MU** and **TSM** premarket strength) reinforce that AI capex and DRAM/HBM demand remain the market's core long-term growth narrative. Finally, with **Marvell** and **Flex** set to join the S&P 500 on June 22, expect continued index-driven buying in **MRVL** this week as passive funds position ahead of the rebalance.
