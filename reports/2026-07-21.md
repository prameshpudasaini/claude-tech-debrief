# 2026-07-21

- **Alibaba jumped 5% after its Qwen AI models won approval for integration into Apple devices sold in China.** The deal makes Alibaba a core AI engine for Apple's local ecosystem, a workaround for Apple's inability to ship its own AI features in China and a meaningful new revenue and validation stream for Alibaba's foundation models.
- **Microsoft and AMD expanded their Azure AI infrastructure partnership, and AMD stock surged 5%.** Microsoft will deploy AMD's new "Helios" rack-scale systems — Instinct MI455X GPUs, 6th-gen EPYC "Venice" CPUs, and Pensando networking — to power frontier-model inference on Azure starting in the second half of 2026, deepening Microsoft's multi-vendor hedge against Nvidia.
- **Tempus AI agreed to acquire cancer-monitoring firm Personalis for about $1.5 billion in stock.** The deal, at $16.25/share (a 6% premium), folds Personalis' molecular residual disease (MRD) testing into Tempus' AI-driven precision oncology platform in what the companies call a $20 billion market opportunity; it's expected to close in early 2027.
- **A federal judge temporarily blocked the $110 billion Paramount–Warner Bros. Discovery merger, and WBD shares fell 3.4%.** A coalition of state attorneys general led by California won a 14-day restraining order over antitrust concerns about theatrical distribution and cable-licensing concentration; a preliminary-injunction hearing is set for August 3, putting the entire deal timeline at risk.
- **Archer Aviation stock ripped 20% higher after unveiling "Thunder," an autonomous attack rotorcraft built with defense-tech firm Anduril.** The dual-use VTOL platform, shown at the Farnborough Airshow, combines Archer's hybrid-electric propulsion with Anduril's Lattice autonomy software and targets a U.S. Department of Defense program of record, with production flights planned for 2027.
- **Blackstone invested roughly $676 million in Futronic, a South Korean maker of high-precision actuators used in robotics and automotive systems.** The deal values Futronic at about 1 trillion won and extends Blackstone's push into industrial-automation supply chains as robotics and humanoid-adjacent hardware demand accelerates.

The common thread today is AI infrastructure and distribution deals still driving single-stock moves even as the broader market wobbles on AI-capex jitters — Microsoft-AMD and Alibaba-Apple both show hyperscalers and device makers locking in multi-year AI supply chains rather than pulling back. Watch AMD, Alibaba (BABA), and Archer (ACHR) for continued momentum, and keep WBD/Paramount on radar since the merger's fate now hinges on the August 3 antitrust hearing. The Archer-Anduril reveal is also a signal that defense-tech convergence (autonomy + electric aviation) is becoming its own investable theme alongside pure AI infrastructure plays.
