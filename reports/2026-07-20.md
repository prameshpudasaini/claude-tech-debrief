# 2026-07-20

- **AMD landed Microsoft as its marquee Helios customer, and the stock jumped ~5% premarket.** Microsoft will deploy AMD's new rack-scale Helios AI systems (Instinct MI455X GPUs + Epyc "Venice" CPUs) across Azure data centers starting in the back half of 2026, joining Meta, OpenAI and Oracle as early Helios buyers — AMD's most direct shot yet at Nvidia's data-center dominance.
- **TSMC tacked on another $100 billion to its Arizona buildout, pushing total US investment to $265 billion.** The move, confirmed alongside a 77% jump in quarterly profit, adds four more advanced fabs (2nm and below) plus packaging and R&D facilities, and comes as the White House and Commerce Department continue pressing TSMC toward a separate joint-venture deal with Intel.
- **Intel and TSMC are still working through a preliminary deal that would hand TSMC a 20% stake in Intel's US fabs.** In exchange TSMC would share manufacturing know-how and training rather than cash; AMD, Broadcom, Nvidia and Qualcomm have reportedly been approached about investing too. Nothing is final, but Intel reports earnings this week and the market will be listening for an update.
- **Meta and Anthropic are in early talks on a compute deal worth up to $10 billion over two years.** It would be Meta's first real step into selling AI infrastructure/cloud capacity and would sit alongside Anthropic's existing $45 billion, three-year SpaceX compute agreement — another sign that compute scarcity, not model quality, is the AI industry's binding constraint right now.
- **Zoom is buying Seattle AI startup Common Room** to bolt an AI-agent-driven sales and marketing platform onto its push to reposition itself as an AI-native workplace tool, rather than a pure video-conferencing name.
- **Apple has reportedly struck a preliminary deal for Intel to manufacture some of its chips**, a potential second-source arrangement alongside TSMC that would reduce Apple's foundry concentration risk and could strengthen its pricing leverage with TSMC.

Today's moves all point to the same story: the AI capex race is shifting from "who has the most Nvidia GPUs" to "who controls enough compute, chip supply, and manufacturing capacity to keep up." AMD's Microsoft win and Apple's reported Intel foundry deal both diversify supply chains away from single vendors (Nvidia, TSMC), while TSMC's bigger Arizona bet and the still-unsettled Intel-TSMC JV talks show the US chipmaking build-out isn't slowing down. Watch AMD and INTC for follow-through this week, TSM and AAPL around the foundry-deal headlines, and META/ANTHROPIC-linked names if the compute deal talks firm up — Intel's earnings later this week could be the next catalyst.
