# July 6, 2026 — Tech Deal Debrief

---

## Key Deals & Moves (Last 24 Hours)

- **TeraWulf signs 20-year, $19B AI data center lease with Anthropic:** The former bitcoin miner will build a 401 MW campus at its Justified Data site in Kentucky dedicated to Anthropic, with contracted revenue of roughly $19 billion over the lease term; TeraWulf simultaneously sold its 50.1% stake in a separate joint venture (Abernathy) to a Fluidstack-led buyer group for about $530 million. WULF shares jumped more than 10% on the news.

- **Broadcom extends custom silicon deal with Apple through 2031:** Broadcom signed new multi-year agreements to design and supply application-specific chips (ASICs) for multiple future generations of Apple devices, locking in a steady revenue stream and easing concerns about losing Apple as a customer. AVGO shares rose about 4%, and the deal lifted the broader semiconductor sector (SMH ETF +2.7%).

- **Ceva wins "landmark" AI chip-licensing deal:** Chip-IP licensor Ceva announced a major agreement with an undisclosed "major U.S. software and AI platform company" to use its NeuPro-M neural processing unit IP in a custom silicon program for on-device generative and agentic AI. CEVA shares gained more than 6%, and the deal signals software platform companies increasingly designing their own AI silicon rather than relying solely on merchant chipmakers.

- **Microsoft cuts roughly 3,200 Xbox jobs:** The cuts, about 20% of the Xbox division, will run through fiscal year 2027 as part of continued restructuring in Microsoft's gaming unit — the latest sign that even large, profitable tech companies are trimming headcount to redirect spending toward AI infrastructure.

- **Foxconn (Hon Hai) posts stronger-than-expected quarterly sales:** Nvidia's key hardware supplier reported sales ahead of estimates, reinforcing that AI server and GPU demand remains robust heading into the next earnings cycle and giving a lift to AI hardware-linked names.

- **SpaceX set to join the Nasdaq-100 index Tuesday:** Following its record-setting June 12 IPO (nearly $2 trillion valuation), SpaceX will be added to the index before trading opens tomorrow — a mechanical move that forces index funds tracking the Nasdaq-100 to buy shares.

---

## Market Synthesis

The clear theme today is AI infrastructure lock-in: TeraWulf's $19B Anthropic lease and Broadcom's extended Apple silicon deal both show operators and chipmakers securing decade-plus contracts to fund and de-risk the AI buildout, while Ceva's licensing win shows even software platform companies are now designing custom silicon. Watch **WULF**, **AVGO**, and **CEVA** for continued momentum, plus the semiconductor ETF (**SMH**) broadly, as data-center and chip-supply deals keep driving sector-specific rallies even as index-level moves stay muted. Microsoft's Xbox layoffs are a reminder that AI capex is increasingly being funded by cuts elsewhere in the business — a pattern likely to recur at other large-cap tech names this earnings season.
