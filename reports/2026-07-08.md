# July 8, 2026 — Tech Deal Debrief

---

## Key Deals & Moves (Last 24 Hours)

- **Apple locks in a $30 billion, multiyear chip deal with Broadcom running through 2031:** The agreement covers custom ASIC silicon, RF components, and Wi-Fi/Bluetooth connectivity across future generations of Apple hardware, and marks Apple's largest commitment yet under its American Manufacturing Program. Broadcom shares jumped as much as 5% on the news and will invest $1.5B expanding its Fort Collins, CO plant to support it.

- **DeepSeek is quietly developing its own AI inference chip, spooking the semiconductor trade:** Reports that the Chinese AI lab has spent roughly a year working with outside chip-design firms and foundries to reduce its reliance on Nvidia sent Nvidia down ~1.5% premarket, with steeper drops in memory names — Micron (-4.7%), Western Digital (-6.3%), and SanDisk (-4.6%).

- **Microsoft guts its Xbox studio portfolio, cutting 3,200 jobs (~20% of gaming staff):** Compulsion Games and Double Fine Productions are being spun off as independent studios with Microsoft runway funding, while Ninja Theory and Undead Labs are being sold to unnamed acquirers. It's the most sweeping Xbox reorg under new gaming CEO Asha Sharma, following years of unprofitable studio acquisitions.

- **Amazon moves to raise another $25 billion via a US dollar bond sale to fund AI infrastructure buildout:** The raise adds to Big Tech's mounting debt load as Alphabet, Amazon, Meta, and Microsoft collectively barrel toward $700B+ in 2026 capex — a scale increasingly financed with borrowed money rather than pure free cash flow.

- **Tesla-SpaceX merger speculation intensifies, though nothing is confirmed:** RBC lifted its Tesla price target to $500, baking in a premium tied to a potential all-stock SpaceX combination that could create a company worth nearly $4 trillion given SpaceX's ~$2.1T private valuation. Wedbush's Dan Ives now puts merger odds above 80% within a year, though prediction markets are far more skeptical (~25% by year-end).

- **Palantir shares remain volatile as investors re-price its Nvidia "sovereign AI" partnership:** PLTR slipped 4.3% as skepticism grows over whether the government/defense AI opportunity — bulled up to a $331B valuation narrative — will convert into real contracts, even as Nvidia continues climbing on the same deal.

---

## Market Synthesis

Chipmaking is the throughline today: Apple-Broadcom shows US Big Tech doubling down on domestic custom silicon, while the DeepSeek chip report is a reminder that Nvidia's moat is being tested from the demand side, not just by rivals like AMD. Watch **AVGO** for follow-through strength, and **NVDA**, **MU**, **WDC** for whether today's dip is a blip or the start of a longer de-rating. Away from chips, Microsoft's Xbox retrenchment and Amazon's fresh borrowing both point to the same theme: Big Tech is getting more disciplined about capital allocation even as AI spending keeps climbing, and investors are starting to demand proof of returns rather than just scale — the Palantir wobble is the clearest example of that shift. The Tesla-SpaceX merger chatter is speculative but market-moving; if confirmed, it would be one of the largest corporate combinations in history, so **TSLA** options activity and further RBC/Wedbush-style target revisions are worth tracking closely.
