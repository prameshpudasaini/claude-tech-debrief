# Tech Deal Debrief — June 3, 2026

## Key Deals & Announcements

- **SpaceX targets $135/share IPO roadshow price, $1.75T valuation** — SpaceX set a fixed $135 roadshow price today, targeting a $75 billion raise and a $1.75 trillion valuation ahead of a June 12 Nasdaq debut under ticker SPCX — which would shatter every prior IPO record by more than 2.5x. Up to 5% of shares are reserved for employees and insiders via a direct share program.

- **Anthropic confidentially files S-1 with SEC** — The Claude maker filed a draft S-1 on June 1 following its $65 billion Series H, which pegged its valuation at ~$965 billion; revenue run-rate hit $47 billion in May alone. An October 2026 Nasdaq listing is the target, and a $1T+ debut is now the base case — putting pressure on Google (GOOGL) and Amazon (AMZN), both major Anthropic investors.

- **Quantinuum sets June 4 IPO at up to $14.3B valuation** — Honeywell's (HON) quantum computing spinoff is raising up to $1.46 billion in its IPO, pricing tomorrow. This is the first major quantum-pure-play to reach public markets at scale.

- **Nvidia + IREN: 5-gigawatt AI factory buildout, $2.1B equity option** — Nvidia received a five-year option to buy up to 30 million IREN shares at $70 each (~$2.1 billion), tying GPU supply directly to data center equity — a model Nvidia has now replicated with CoreWeave and Nebius for 10+ gigawatts of committed capacity.

- **Fivetran + dbt Labs merger closes** — The all-stock deal between the two data-pipeline leaders officially completed, creating a unified "Open Data Infrastructure for AI" platform backed by Thoma Bravo. Signals continued consolidation in the enterprise data/AI tooling layer.

- **AMD + Meta finalize 6-gigawatt GPU supply agreement** — AMD locked in a multi-generational commitment to power Meta's AI infrastructure with custom Instinct MI450 GPUs and EPYC CPUs; first gigawatt shipments begin H2 2026, reinforcing AMD's (AMD) position as Nvidia's most credible hyperscaler alternative.

- **Accenture invests in AlphaSense AI intelligence platform** — Accenture (ACN) announced a strategic investment and partnership to embed AlphaSense's 500M-document AI market intelligence platform into client workflows across financial services, healthcare, and tech.

## Synthesis

The dominant theme this week is AI infrastructure financing at historic scale — from Nvidia effectively taking equity stakes in data center operators (IREN, CoreWeave) to the two largest potential IPOs in history (SpaceX, Anthropic) both in active motion simultaneously. The SpaceX roadshow starting this week will pull significant institutional attention and liquidity toward growth tech, creating both opportunity and near-term pressure on existing large-cap names. Watch **NVDA** (infrastructure leverage), **HON** (Quantinuum parent, IPO catalyst), **AMZN and GOOGL** (Anthropic investors with pre-IPO marks to reprice), and **AMD** (Meta GPU supply clarity). The Anthropic filing, in particular, sharpens the competitive stakes for every AI platform stock — its $47B annualized revenue run-rate, if confirmed in the S-1, will force a revaluation conversation across the sector.
