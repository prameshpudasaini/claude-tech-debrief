# 2026-07-16

- **Stripe and Advent International offer $53 billion to buy PayPal.** The $60.50-per-share all-cash-and-stock proposal — backed by ~$50B in committed bank financing and equity from Stripe, Advent, and Block — sent PayPal shares up 17% on record volume; PayPal's board, advised by Goldman Sachs and Evercore, is set to meet as soon as July 20 to respond, making this the largest fintech takeover ever attempted.
- **TSMC beats Q2 estimates and raises its 2026 outlook, but the stock still falls.** Revenue grew 36% year-over-year with AI chips now 61% of sales, and TSMC lifted full-year revenue growth guidance to "slightly more than 40%" and 2026 capex to $60-64 billion — yet shares dropped over 4%, dragging the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index down more than 2% on renewed doubts about whether AI infrastructure spending will pay off.
- **SK Hynix shares tumble another 11% today**, extending last week's record one-day plunge as fallout continues from its decision to slow HBM4 production in favor of higher-margin conventional DRAM — the memory-sector wobble is spilling over into Micron and Western Digital.
- **Onsemi's $7 billion all-stock bid for Synaptics is moving toward a shareholder vote**, with fresh SEC merger filings this week; the deal would fold Synaptics' edge-AI and connectivity silicon into Onsemi's power business as chipmakers race to own "physical AI" hardware.
- **Alibaba's Qwen AI model clears Chinese regulatory approval to run on Apple devices sold in China**, deepening Apple's AI partner stack in its largest overseas market; Alibaba's US-listed shares rose about 5% on the news.

Today's tape shows two competing forces: real fintech and semiconductor M&A (PayPal's $53B bid, Onsemi-Synaptics) pushing forward even as the broader chip trade wobbles on AI-capex-payoff anxiety, with TSMC's beat-and-raise failing to stop a sector-wide slide and SK Hynix extending its slump. Watch **PYPL** into the July 20 board meeting for the next leg of the takeover fight, and watch **TSM**, **SK Hynix (SKHYY)**, **MU**, and the semiconductor index for whether this is a healthy pause or the start of a deeper AI-valuation reset. Onsemi (**ON**) and Synaptics (**SYNA**) are also worth tracking as that deal works through the approval process.
