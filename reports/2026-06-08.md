# June 8, 2026 — Tech Market Debrief

## Major Deals & Moves (Past 24 Hours)

- **SpaceX barrels toward the largest IPO in history:** The roadshow is wrapping up with pricing expected after the close on June 11 and Nasdaq trading (ticker: SPCX) set to begin June 12. SpaceX is targeting $135/share for a ~$1.77 trillion valuation and a $75 billion raise — a deal so large it's reportedly already pulling investor capital out of other tech names ahead of its debut.

- **Chip stocks suffer a "Black Friday" rout, erasing ~$1 trillion:** Broadcom plunged roughly 14% after guiding Q3 AI-chip sales to $16B (below the $17.2B Street estimate) and declining to raise its full-year AI forecast, dragging down Marvell (-16%), Intel and AMD (~-11%), and Micron (-13%). The Nasdaq fell 4% — its worst day since April 2025 — on the combined weight of the Broadcom miss, a SemiAnalysis report on Nvidia trimming memory specs, and capital rotating toward SpaceX's mega-IPO.

- **Anthropic presses its IPO lead over OpenAI:** Following its confidential S-1 filing, Anthropic disclosed it raised $65 billion at a $965 billion valuation — vaulting past OpenAI's last $852 billion mark. Analysts at Wedbush say the move could "open the floodgates" for a wave of AI-company listings (Anthropic, OpenAI, SpaceX) later this year.

- **Quantum Space to go public via $1.2B SPAC merger:** The space-defense and orbital-mobility company agreed to combine with Inflection Point Acquisition Corp. VI, including a $300M PIPE, and will trade on Nasdaq as "QSPC" once the deal closes in Q4 2026.

- **Factorial debuts on Nasdaq:** The solid-state battery developer completed its SPAC combination with Cartesian Growth Corporation III, implying roughly a $1.3 billion equity value, and began trading June 8 under tickers FAC/FACWW.

- **Navitas Semiconductor jumps on new Nvidia partnership:** Shares surged 20-25% after Navitas unveiled a collaboration with Nvidia to co-develop 800-volt DC power architecture for next-generation AI data centers (unveiled at Computex), extending a 2026 rally that has the stock up more than 340% year-to-date.

- **Intel breakup chatter resurfaces:** Reports continue to circulate that TSMC and Broadcom — with Qualcomm also reportedly interested — are weighing deals to split up Intel's foundry and chip-design businesses. Talks remain informal with no offers submitted, but any formal move would reshuffle the entire chip industry.

---

## Market Synthesis

The dominant theme is capital rotation: as SpaceX's record-shattering IPO (with Anthropic right behind it) sucks investor attention and cash toward fresh listings, established AI-chip leaders — Broadcom, Micron, Marvell, Intel, AMD — got hammered on guidance worries and supply-chain jitters, a reminder that even the hottest "AI winners" aren't immune to a sharp reset. Watch **AVGO, MU, MRVL, and INTC** for continued volatility in the coming days, and keep an eye on **SPCX** once it starts trading June 12 — a stumble in the largest IPO ever could ripple through broader risk sentiment. At the same time, money is still flowing into AI-infrastructure niches (Navitas's Nvidia tie-up, fresh SPAC listings in batteries and space tech), and the long-simmering Intel breakup speculation could become a major catalyst for **INTC, AVGO, TSM, and QCOM** if any of those informal talks turn into real bids.
