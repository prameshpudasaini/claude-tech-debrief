# June 10, 2026 — Tech Market Debrief

## Major Deals & Moves (Past 24 Hours)

- **Anthropic ships Claude Fable 5 and Claude Mythos 5 (June 9):** Anthropic publicly released its first "Mythos-class" models — Fable 5 (with extra safeguards) and Mythos 5 — claiming over 10% gains in agentic coding, knowledge work, and cybersecurity benchmarks. The launch lands days after Anthropic's confidential IPO filing and raises the competitive bar for OpenAI and Google just as the AI-IPO wave builds.

- **Apollo and Blackstone finalize $35B AI chip financing for Anthropic (June 8):** The two firms closed a $35B private-credit package — backed by credit guarantees from **Broadcom (AVGO)** — to fund a special-purpose vehicle that buys Google's custom TPUs and leases them to Anthropic. The deal deepens ties between Anthropic, Google, and Broadcom's balance sheet just as Anthropic preps its own listing.

- **Broadcom's guidance miss erases over $1T from AI chip stocks (June 9–10):** Broadcom posted Q2 AI revenue of $10.8B (+143% YoY) but guided Q3 AI chip sales to ~$16B, about $1.2B below estimates. The miss sent **Nvidia (NVDA)**, **AMD**, **Micron (MU)**, and **Intel (INTC)** sharply lower, with **Oracle's** earnings after today's close seen as the next test of AI-infrastructure spending confidence.

- **Software stocks slide on AI-disruption fears (June 9):** **Palantir (PLTR)**, **Oracle (ORCL)**, and **Workday (WDAY)** all fell after Anthropic's Fable 5 launch reignited investor worry that frontier AI models will erode demand for traditional enterprise software.

- **Nvidia acquires enterprise AI startup Kumo AI for $400M+ (June 4):** Nvidia bought Kumo AI, a predictive-AI startup backed by Sequoia that builds foundation models for customer-churn and credit-risk predictions — another step in Nvidia's push to own the full enterprise AI software stack.

- **SpaceX IPO enters its final stretch (debut June 12):** SpaceX holds a dedicated retail-investor event on June 11 ahead of its Friday Nasdaq debut as **SPCX**, priced at $135/share for a $1.77T valuation — the largest IPO in history, raising roughly $75B.

---

## Market Synthesis

The AI infrastructure buildout keeps accelerating on the financing side — Apollo/Blackstone's $35B chip deal for Anthropic and Nvidia's Kumo acquisition show capital and consolidation still flowing freely — but public markets are growing skittish about the payoff. Broadcom's soft guidance wiped out more than $1 trillion in chip-stock value and dragged software names like Palantir, Oracle, and Workday down too, as Anthropic's powerful new Fable 5 model stokes fears of AI cannibalizing software incumbents. **Oracle's earnings tonight** are the key near-term catalyst: a beat could stabilize NVDA, AMD, AVGO, and MU, while a miss would deepen the selloff. Separately, **SpaceX's record-breaking SPCX debut on Friday** is likely to dominate headlines regardless of how the chip story plays out — watch for spillover sentiment into aerospace and satellite names. Net-net: AI capex conviction remains intact among big balance sheets, but equity investors are starting to demand proof of returns.
