# June 17, 2026 — Tech Market Debrief

## Major Deals & Moves (Past 24 Hours)

- **Coherent (COHR) wins $50M CHIPS Act LOI to expand AI optics manufacturing:** The U.S. Commerce Department announced a letter of intent on June 16 to invest up to $50M in Coherent's indium phosphide fab in Sherman, Texas — the facility will double its floor space and quadruple wafer capacity for the optical networking chips that power Nvidia data centers, deepening Coherent's already-strategic supply relationship with Nvidia and supporting over 1,000 new jobs.

- **Global semiconductor equipment sales hit a record $36.55B in Q1 2026:** New data released June 16 showed the semiconductor equipment market surpassed all prior quarterly records in Q1, driven by AI-related spending on advanced logic, high-bandwidth memory (HBM), and advanced packaging; Applied Materials, ASML, Lam Research, and KLA are the primary beneficiaries — Applied Materials alone is guiding for 20%+ revenue growth in calendar 2026.

- **SpaceX (SPCX) trades at ~$193, giving it a $2.6T market cap six days post-IPO:** After pricing at $135 and closing up 19% on its June 12 debut — the largest IPO in history at $75B raised — SPCX pulled back slightly from a $201 Monday close but continues to hold well above its offering price, with a market cap now exceeding Tesla's and making it the fifth-largest public company in the world.

- **IQM Quantum Computers SPAC merger clears SEC, shareholder vote set for June 25:** The SEC declared IQM's F-4 registration statement effective, advancing the merger with Real Asset Acquisition Corp. (RAAQ, Nasdaq); if shareholders approve on June 25, IQM will list under ticker IQMX at a $1.8B valuation — becoming the first publicly traded European quantum computing company — backed by a €50M BlackRock investment and access to roughly $175M from RAAQ's trust.

- **Defense tech startups raise a record $14.6B in 2026 as Anduril eyes IPO:** Crunchbase and TechTimes data shows autonomous-weapons and AI-defense startups are on track to shatter prior full-year funding records, paced by Anduril's May Series H at a $61B valuation led by Thrive Capital and a16z; both Anduril and Shield AI are now widely cited as the most likely near-term IPO candidates in defense tech.

- **OpenAI files confidentially for IPO, setting up a possible fall 2026 listing:** Following SpaceX's record debut, OpenAI filed confidential IPO paperwork with the SEC at an estimated $800B–$1T+ valuation; the company cautioned a listing may still be "a while away" but the filing puts it in direct competition with Anthropic — which filed the prior week at a ~$965B valuation — for the next landmark AI public offering.

- **Figure Technology acquires Kiavi for $717M, deepening AI-fintech convergence:** Figure Technology Solutions, in a joint venture with Sixth Street, agreed to acquire Kiavi — an AI-powered lending platform for residential real estate investors — in a $717M deal that positions Figure's blockchain-native marketplace against traditional mortgage originators as AI-driven underwriting accelerates.

---

## Market Synthesis

Today's dominant theme is **infrastructure and the IPO pipeline closing in**. The Coherent CHIPS Act award and the Q1 equipment-spending record both confirm that the physical buildout of AI data centers is a multi-year structural capex cycle — not a single surge — and investors should stay overweight on picks-and-shovels plays: **COHR**, **AMAT**, **ASML**, **LRCX**, and **KLAC**. Meanwhile, **SPCX**'s post-IPO hold above its offering price is being watched as a litmus test for whether mega-cap AI stocks can sustain rich multiples without a near-term earnings catalyst. The most actionable binary event ahead is **IQM's June 25 RAAQ shareholder vote**, which is a read-across for quantum computing sentiment broadly. Longer-term, the **OpenAI/Anthropic dual IPO pipeline** — both now filing in the same week — could be the largest new-equity event of the second half and a catalyst for re-rating the entire generative AI sector; watch for formal S-1 filings in July or August as the next major signal.
