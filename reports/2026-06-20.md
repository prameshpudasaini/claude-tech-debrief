# June 20, 2026 — Tech Deal Debrief

---

## Key Deals & Moves (Last 24–48 Hours)

- **SpaceX acquires Cursor AI for $60 billion (June 16)** — Just four days after its record-breaking $75B Nasdaq IPO (ticker: SPCX), SpaceX agreed to buy Anysphere's AI coding assistant Cursor in an all-stock deal — the largest acquisition of a VC-backed startup in history. Cursor was last valued at ~$9B and had reached $4B in annualized revenue.

- **SpaceX IPO on Nasdaq (June 12)** — SpaceX went public at $150/share and quickly surged past a $2 trillion market cap, surpassing the largest IPO in stock market history. The listing instantly makes SPCX one of the most closely watched new entrants in the mega-cap space.

- **Fox Corporation acquires Roku for $22 billion (June 15)** — Fox agreed to buy Roku at $160/share in a cash-and-stock deal, combining Fox's live sports, news, and Tubi streaming service with Roku's 100M+ connected TV households. The deal is expected to close with ~$400M in run-rate cost synergies.

- **Qualcomm in talks to acquire Tenstorrent for up to $10 billion (June 16)** — Qualcomm is reportedly in advanced negotiations to buy AI chip startup Tenstorrent (founded by legendary chip architect Jim Keller), which builds scalable AI accelerators on a RISC-V architecture. The move would directly challenge Nvidia in edge and data center AI inference.

- **Quantinuum IPO on Nasdaq (June 4)** — Quantum computing company Quantinuum (ticker: QNT) raised $1.68B in its Nasdaq debut, priced at $60/share. The listing signals renewed investor appetite for deep-tech hardware bets as AI spending lifts interest in adjacent compute technologies.

- **NVIDIA and SK Hynix announce multiyear memory partnership** — The two companies formalized a technology partnership to co-develop next-generation HBM and advanced memory specifically architected for Nvidia's AI factory buildout, applying AI to chip design and manufacturing processes including autonomous fab operations.

- **Intel's 18A-P node enters risk production; Apple and Google reported as interested customers** — Intel's leading-edge 18A-P process node has entered risk production, with reports surfacing that both Apple and Google are evaluating it as a foundry option. A strategic partnership with Taiwan's UMC was also announced to expand Intel Foundry Services capacity.

## Market Synthesis

This week's deal flow is being shaped by two colliding forces: the post-SpaceX-IPO momentum unlocking a new wave of mega-cap AI spending, and an intensifying race to lock up AI chip supply chains before Nvidia's lead becomes insurmountable. SpaceX's immediate $60B Cursor acquisition signals that newly public AI-adjacent companies will use their inflated equity as acquisition currency aggressively — watch for similar moves from other freshly listed tech names. The Qualcomm–Tenstorrent talks are the clearest sign yet that legacy chip companies are making desperate, expensive bets to avoid being cut out of the AI inference market. Meanwhile, Fox's Roku deal resets the floor for streaming distribution valuations and puts pressure on Comcast, Charter, and Amazon to respond. Stocks to watch: **SPCX**, **ROKU**, **QCOM**, **NVDA**, **AMD**, **INTC**, and **QNT**.
