# 2026-07-17

- **TSMC pledges another $100 billion for U.S. chip plants, pushing its total commitment to $265 billion.** The Trump administration secured the increase — four more advanced fabs and packaging facilities in Arizona focused on 2nm chips — following January's US-Taiwan trade deal; it reinforces the reshoring trend investors have been rewarding in chip-equipment and construction names.
- **Uber offers $14.8 billion (€41.50/share) to acquire Delivery Hero, aiming to nearly double its global footprint.** Combined with Uber's existing ~25% stake and derivative exposure, its economic interest would hit ~53%; the deal would create the largest food-delivery group outside China across 99 countries, with Delivery Hero's board unanimously backing the offer.
- **Apple expands its chip-supply partnership with Broadcom in a new six-year deal** for custom silicon and wireless connectivity components built in Fort Collins, Colorado — extending the companies' existing multiyear tie-up and Apple's broader U.S. manufacturing push.
- **NetApp acquires DataPelago**, a California-based AI data-infrastructure startup that eliminates processing bottlenecks for AI and analytics workloads, adding GPU-accelerated data pipeline capability to NetApp's storage stack.
- **Csquare (data-center REIT-style operator spun out of Brookfield) priced its IPO at $21/share, raising $1.05 billion.** The listing is being compared to Equinix and Digital Realty but priced at a discount given thinner margins and no dividend — a read on investor appetite for pure-play AI data-center exposure.
- **Chinese AI lab Moonshot released Kimi K3, a 2.8-trillion-parameter open-weight model that beat several US frontier models on coding and agentic benchmarks**, though it still trails top-tier US models overall. The release triggered a "DeepSeek moment" selloff: Nvidia, Micron, Broadcom, and Qualcomm each fell over 2% premarket, while AMD, Intel, and Marvell dropped more than 3%.

Today's dominant story is the tension between AI infrastructure buildout and AI infrastructure anxiety: TSMC and Apple/Broadcom are doubling down on U.S. chip capacity even as Moonshot's cheap, capable open-weight model reignited fears that hyperscalers' massive capex may not be as defensible as priced in, hammering Nvidia, AMD, Micron, Broadcom, and Qualcomm in early trading. Outside chips, consolidation continues in adjacent sectors — Uber's Delivery Hero bid signals a wave of platform-scale roll-ups in food delivery, while Csquare's IPO tests whether investors still want to pay up for AI data-center real estate at a discount to legacy REITs. Watch semiconductor names (NVDA, AMD, MU, AVGO, QCOM, INTC, MRVL) for continued weakness if the Kimi K3 story has legs, and keep an eye on TSMC/Apple suppliers as the reshoring capex story plays out alongside it.
