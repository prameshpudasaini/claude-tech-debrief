# June 7, 2026 — Tech Market Debrief

## Major Deals & Moves (Past 24 Hours)

- **SpaceX inks ~$30B cloud compute deal with Google:** Per an SEC filing made public June 5–6, Google will pay SpaceX roughly $920M/month for three years (Oct 2026–June 2029) for access to ~110,000 Nvidia GPUs and related infrastructure at SpaceX/xAI's Colossus data centers. The deal hands SpaceX a major new revenue stream just ahead of its June 12 IPO and underscores Google's scramble to lock up scarce AI compute capacity.

- **OpenAI moves to finalize Wall Street IPO paperwork:** Reports out today say OpenAI — working with Goldman Sachs and Morgan Stanley — is racing to complete its confidential listing filing, putting it neck-and-neck with Anthropic (which filed June 1) in a sprint toward a public debut that could land as early as September at a valuation north of $1 trillion.

- **Trump floats US government equity stakes in OpenAI, Anthropic, and xAI:** President Trump told reporters Friday that Washington may take direct ownership positions in major AI labs, a policy shift that — paired with Sen. Sanders' proposed 50% "windfall" stock tax on frontier AI firms — adds fresh political risk to AI valuations heading into the IPO wave.

- **Apple heads into WWDC with a blockbuster AI reveal teed up:** Apple's developer conference opens Monday (June 8) with the unveiling of a rebuilt, Gemini-powered Siri (running on a custom 1.2-trillion-parameter Google model under Apple's ~$1B/year licensing deal) plus a new "Extensions" framework letting iPhone users choose Claude, ChatGPT, or Gemini to power Apple Intelligence — a potential AAPL/GOOGL catalyst as markets open this week.

- **Liftoff debuts on Nasdaq as LFTO:** The mobile ad-tech company priced its IPO at $23/share, raising $437M, and began trading this week — a fresh data point on investor appetite for mid-sized tech listings even as the broader IPO pipeline (SpaceX, Anthropic, OpenAI) dominates headlines.

- **Apple–Intel chip deal continues to lift INTC:** Reports that Apple and Intel reached an agreement in principle for Intel to manufacture future iPhone/Mac processors keep fueling Intel's stock (+240% YTD), validating its foundry turnaround and reshaping the Apple–TSMC supply-chain dynamic.

## Market Synthesis

The throughline this morning is that AI infrastructure and AI distribution deals are now the market's main event — Google is paying SpaceX tens of billions for raw compute while simultaneously paying Apple roughly $1B a year just to power Siri, showing how every major platform is buying its way into the AI stack from multiple angles at once. The IPO race is also heating into a genuine sprint, with OpenAI, Anthropic, and SpaceX all jockeying for a 2026 listing window that could collectively bring over $200B in new public supply. Stocks to watch this week: **GOOGL** (new SpaceX compute revenue plus its expanding Gemini licensing footprint), **AAPL** (WWDC's Gemini-Siri reveal is a real near-term catalyst), **NVDA** (the SpaceX-Google deal is built entirely on its GPUs), and **INTC** (foundry momentum from the reported Apple deal). The wildcard is Washington — Trump's equity-stake idea and Sanders' proposed AI windfall tax inject a new layer of policy risk that could introduce volatility into AI-adjacent names just as the IPO window opens.
