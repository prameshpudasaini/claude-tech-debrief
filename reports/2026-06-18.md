# June 18, 2026 — Tech Market Debrief

## Major Deals & Moves (Past 24 Hours)

- **SpaceX acquires AI coding startup Cursor for $60 billion in largest-ever VC-backed acquisition:** Just days after its record IPO on Nasdaq (SPCX), SpaceX announced an all-stock deal to buy Anysphere, the company behind AI coding assistant Cursor, in what Crunchbase confirms is the largest acquisition of a venture-backed startup on record; SpaceX is betting that Cursor — which lets developers write and debug code via natural-language prompts — gives it the AI software edge that xAI failed to build.

- **Accenture acquires Dragos, runZero, and NetRise for $4.18 billion in major OT cybersecurity bet:** Accenture announced the purchase of three industrial cybersecurity firms in a single deal targeting the protection of critical infrastructure — power grids, pipelines, and manufacturing facilities — tapping into a $27 billion OT security market that Accenture projects will grow to nearly $59 billion by 2031; the three companies collectively generate ~$208M in ARR, up 53% year-over-year.

- **Microsoft and OpenAI formally end their exclusivity agreement:** OpenAI and Microsoft restructured their landmark partnership, with OpenAI now free to run its services on Amazon Web Services and Google Cloud in addition to Azure; Microsoft retains its position as "primary cloud partner" and will continue receiving a revenue share through 2030, but the move meaningfully widens OpenAI's cloud options and introduces competitive pressure into Microsoft's Azure lock-in thesis.

- **Anthropic IPO filing moves closer to public disclosure:** Anthropic — the maker of Claude and current enterprise AI market leader with annualized revenue exceeding $44 billion — has filed a confidential S-1 with the SEC, with a Nasdaq or NYSE listing targeted as early as Q4 2026; the filing follows OpenAI's own confidential IPO submission, meaning two of the most valuable private AI companies in history are now in the public-markets pipeline simultaneously.

- **Meta signs multi-billion-dollar TPU lease with Google to diversify AI chip supply:** Meta formalized a major agreement with Google to access its Tensor Processing Units for AI workloads, adding Google's custom silicon alongside the company's existing Nvidia GPU and AMD Instinct commitments; the deal signals that Meta is actively reducing its single-supplier dependence on Nvidia even as the broader hyperscaler capex cycle accelerates toward $650 billion in 2026.

- **Ondas Holdings acquires drone inspection firm Cyberhawk for $125 million:** In a mostly cash deal, autonomous solutions company Ondas acquired Cyberhawk, a UK-based leader in aerial inspection for oil-and-gas and energy infrastructure; Cyberhawk is expected to contribute over $45 million in revenue to Ondas's fiscal 2027, significantly scaling the company's industrial drone services footprint.

- **Nasdaq and S&P 500 pull back on Fed rate-hike signals:** Markets sold off on June 17 — Nasdaq -1.34%, S&P 500 -1.21% — as Federal Reserve commentary pointed toward a potential rate hike in late 2026, reversing the prior session's record-high momentum; the pullback is being watched as a test of whether the AI and IPO-driven rally has priced in enough macro risk heading into the second half of the year.

---

## Market Synthesis

The dominant story right now is **SpaceX (SPCX) as the new market force**: less than a week after the largest IPO in history, the company is already deploying its inflated stock currency to make transformative acquisitions — buying Cursor for $60B establishes SPCX not just as an aerospace play but as a direct AI software competitor to Anthropic and OpenAI, both of which are themselves racing toward public listings. The **cybersecurity layer** is also consolidating fast — Accenture's $4.18B Dragos-led acquisition follows Google's $32B Wiz close earlier this year, and with AI creating new industrial attack surfaces, OT security names deserve a close look. The **OpenAI-Microsoft exclusivity unwind** is the quiet restructuring worth tracking for its second-order effects: it opens the door for AWS and Google to capture more AI inference revenue, and it weakens one of Azure's most powerful moats just as the cloud giants are spending $650B+ on AI infrastructure. Stocks to watch: **SPCX**, **ACN**, **MSFT**, **GOOGL**, **AMZN**, and any OT cybersecurity names tied to the Dragos ecosystem.
