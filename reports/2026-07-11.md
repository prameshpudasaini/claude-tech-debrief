# July 11, 2026 — Tech Deal Debrief

---

## Key Deals & Moves (Last 24 Hours)

- **Apple sues OpenAI for alleged trade secret theft tied to its consumer hardware push:** The federal lawsuit accuses OpenAI's hardware chief Tang Tan (a former Apple VP) and ex-Apple engineer Chang Liu of funneling confidential Apple designs into OpenAI's device efforts, which trace back to its $6.4B acquisition of Jony Ive's startup io Products. It marks a stunning breakdown between two companies that were ChatGPT-on-iPhone partners just two years ago, and raises legal risk around OpenAI's hardware roadmap.

- **Meta rallies nearly 6% Friday, up more than 14% on the week, on its custom AI chip progress:** Reports confirmed Meta's next-generation in-house AI chip — co-designed with Broadcom and manufactured by TSMC — starts production in September, aimed at cutting Meta's reliance on Nvidia GPUs amid an ongoing component shortage. The move adds Meta to the growing list of hyperscalers racing to build custom silicon.

- **TeraWulf lines up a $3.5 billion debt raise to build an Anthropic-leased AI data center in Kentucky:** The financing backs a 401-megawatt campus in Hawesville under a 20-year lease with Anthropic worth roughly $19 billion in contracted revenue, with Morgan Stanley expected to lead the bond and loan package. TeraWulf shares fell 5.3% on the capital-raise news even as the underlying AI infrastructure deal remains a major long-term revenue lock-in.

- **Perfect Corp. agrees to go private for $2.00/share in a deal with its chairwoman's holding entity, ProjectNY:** The AI/AR beauty-tech company's buyout represents a roughly 48% premium to its pre-announcement price, backed by voting support from holders controlling over 81% of voting power; the deal is expected to close in Q4 2026 and would delist PERF from the NYSE.

- **Nvidia and SK Hynix announce a multiyear partnership to co-develop next-generation memory for AI data centers:** The deal deepens Nvidia's supply-chain ties to SK Hynix just a day after the chipmaker's record $26.5 billion Nasdaq debut, reinforcing memory as a critical AI infrastructure chokepoint. Nvidia shares rose about 4% Friday on continued AI infrastructure momentum.

- **Qualcomm unveils new data-center AI chips designed to run without high-bandwidth memory, directly challenging Nvidia:** The company is targeting $15 billion in data-center segment revenue by 2029, positioning itself as a lower-cost alternative in AI inference just as memory scarcity squeezes rivals.

## Market Synthesis

Friday's action pushed the S&P 500 (+0.42% to 7,575.39) and Nasdaq (+0.29% to 26,281.61) to fresh highs, with Meta and Nvidia doing the heavy lifting on custom-silicon and memory-partnership news. The throughline across today's deals is hyperscalers and chipmakers racing to secure AI compute and memory supply through vertical moves — Meta building its own chips, Nvidia locking in memory partnerships, TeraWulf raising billions to house Anthropic's compute — while Qualcomm tries to undercut that stack with cheaper, HBM-free inference chips. Watch **META** for follow-through on its Broadcom/TSMC chip news, **SKHY** and **NVDA** as the new memory partnership beds in ahead of Monday's full ticker conversion, and **QCOM** as a potential value play if its no-HBM approach gains traction. The Apple-OpenAI lawsuit is the wildcard: it's a legal story for now, but it injects fresh uncertainty into OpenAI's hardware ambitions and is worth monitoring for any spillover into Apple's own AI roadmap or stock (**AAPL**).
