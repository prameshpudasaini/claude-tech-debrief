# June 13, 2026 — Tech Market Debrief

## Major Deals & Moves (Past 24 Hours)

- **SpaceX (SPCX) extends its post-IPO surge into a second day:** After pricing at $135/share and closing its Nasdaq debut up ~19% at $161 on Friday, SpaceX shares pushed as high as $176.52 on Monday before settling near $161 — keeping the company's valuation around $2T+. MSCI confirmed SPCX will get early "fast-entry" inclusion in its indexes starting June 13, which should pull in passive fund buying.

- **Jeff Bezos' industrial-AI startup Prometheus raises $12B at a $41B valuation:** The round, led by Bezos himself alongside JPMorgan, Goldman Sachs, BlackRock, DST Global, and Arch Venture Partners, brings Prometheus's total funding above $18B. The company is building an "artificial general engineer" to automate design and manufacturing of physical products — from jet engines to semiconductors.

- **Nvidia, Amazon and Qualcomm back German humanoid-robot maker NEURA Robotics in a $1.4B round:** The Series C, which also includes Tether, Bosch, Schaeffler and the European Investment Bank, values NEURA at roughly $7B and is earmarked to scale production toward 5 million robots by 2030 — another sign Big Tech is racing to lock in stakes across the physical-AI/robotics supply chain.

- **Chinese humanoid-robot maker EngineAI confidentially files for a Hong Kong IPO:** The Shenzhen firm, fresh off a $200M Series B at a $1.5B valuation (led by Henan CICC Huirong and Apple/Tesla supplier Luxshare-ICT), is working with CICC and Citic Securities on the listing — adding to a wave of Asian robotics names looking to tap public markets.

- **SpaceX's blowout debut accelerates the Anthropic/OpenAI IPO race:** With Anthropic (filed June 1, post a $65B raise at ~$965B valuation) and OpenAI (filed June 8, ~$852B valuation) both sitting on confidential S-1s, analysts say SpaceX's strong reception makes it more likely both AI labs push toward 2026 listings — though some pension funds have flagged "concentration risk" concerns about three mega-cap tech IPOs landing in the same window.

- **Nvidia keeps deploying its balance sheet as an "AI banker":** Building on its recent equity stakes in Corning (up to $3.2B in warrants) and AI-cloud/data-center operator IREN (up to $2.1B, plus a 5GW infrastructure and cloud-services partnership), Nvidia continues using direct investments to secure supply-chain and compute-capacity partners — a pattern likely to keep showing up in chip-sector trading.

---

## Market Synthesis

The IPO floodgates that SpaceX just kicked open are the story of the week: its second straight day of gains and early MSCI index inclusion signal strong, durable demand rather than a one-day pop, which materially raises the odds Anthropic and OpenAI press ahead with their own 2026 listings — watch **SPCX** for index-driven flows and any pension-fund "diversification" pushback as a preview of how the next mega-IPOs might trade. Beneath that headline, capital continues pouring into physical AI and robotics (Bezos's Prometheus, NEURA Robotics, EngineAI), with **Nvidia, Amazon, and Qualcomm** all showing up as repeat strategic investors — a trend worth tracking for **NVDA**, **AMZN**, and **QCOM** as it could presage more direct-investment-for-supply-chain deals. Overall tone remains risk-on for AI infrastructure and big-tech balance-sheet deployment, even as last week's chip-sector volatility (AVGO, MU) suggests valuations remain sensitive to any disappointment in the upcoming AI-IPO wave.
