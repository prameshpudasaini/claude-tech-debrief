# 2026-07-22

- **AMD kicked off its "Advancing AI 2026" event in San Francisco, unveiling Zen 6 "Venice" EPYC CPUs (the first x86 server chip on TSMC's 2nm process) and the Helios rack powered by MI455X GPUs with 31TB of HBM4.** Executives from Meta, OpenAI, xAI, Oracle, Microsoft, Cohere, and HUMAIN joined the event, reinforcing AMD's push to lock in hyperscaler customers as a direct Nvidia challenger — even as AMD shares fell ~3.5% premarket amid a broader chip selloff.
- **Super Micro Computer stock surged as much as 16-20% after the company said it closed fiscal Q4 with a record $60 billion in new AI-server orders and guided gross margin to 15-17%, well above prior estimates of 8.2-8.4%.** The preliminary results signal AI-infrastructure demand is still outrunning capacity concerns, and the news lifted sentiment across hardware peers like Dell and HPE.
- **OpenAI added Nubank CEO David Vélez and BNY CEO Robin Vince to its board, with Vince set to chair the audit committee, as it builds public-company governance ahead of a potential 2027 IPO.** The move follows OpenAI's S-1 filing in June and signals the IPO push is accelerating, with the company recruiting finance-industry heavyweights rather than more AI researchers.
- **SK Hynix publicly denied a Korean media report that it was in talks to buy Intel's roughly 1,000-acre, $100 billion Ohio chip campus for U.S. memory production.** The denial, filed in a same-day regulatory disclosure, whipsawed SK Hynix shares and underscores how sensitive the memory/foundry sector is to any hint of consolidation around Intel's stalled Ohio buildout.
- **Chip stocks sold off again — KLA Corp fell over 3.6%, AMD dropped 3.5%, and Intel slid over 3% premarket — despite strong quarterly results from ASML and TSMC.** TSMC beat Q2 estimates with 77% net-income growth and raised its 2026 capex guidance to $60-64 billion (plus another $100 billion for Arizona), but investors are increasingly questioning whether AI capex at this scale can be justified, extending a selloff that has cut the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index over 20% from its June peak.
- **Markets are bracing for after-the-bell earnings from Alphabet and Tesla today, the most closely watched reports of this earnings season.** Wall Street is watching whether Google Cloud can sustain 60%+ growth and whether Alphabet's AI spending is starting to pay off, a verdict that will likely set the tone for AI-infrastructure stocks into next week.

The dominant theme remains a tug-of-war between AI infrastructure supply (chip capacity, capex, hyperscaler orders) and investor fatigue over valuations — Super Micro's blowout order book and AMD's hyperscaler-packed product launch argue demand is real, while the chip-sector selloff and the SK Hynix denial show how twitchy the market has gotten around anything resembling AI overbuild or M&A speculation. Watch SMCI and AMD for continued volatility around today's event, and keep OpenAI-adjacent names in view as the IPO narrative firms up with credible financial-sector board hires. Alphabet's and Tesla's earnings after the close are the next major catalyst — a soft Google Cloud print or AI-capex commentary could deepen the chip-sector pullback, while strength there could snap the recent semiconductor slide.
