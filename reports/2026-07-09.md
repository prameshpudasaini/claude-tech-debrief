# July 9, 2026 — Tech Deal Debrief

---

## Key Deals & Moves (Last 24 Hours)

- **Micron raises its US investment pledge to $250 billion through 2035, breaks ground early on its New York megafab:** The chipmaker poured first concrete at its Clay, NY facility ahead of schedule and lifted its total domestic buildout commitment from $200B to $250B, betting on sustained AI-driven memory demand. Shares jumped roughly 8% and the move lifted the broader semiconductor sector.

- **SK Hynix prices its $28 billion Nasdaq listing after demand ran 7x oversubscribed:** The South Korean memory giant's US offering — backed by cornerstone commitments from Baillie Gifford, Coatue Management, and Situational Awareness Partners totaling up to $7B — is set to become the largest-ever US listing by a foreign company, eclipsing Alibaba's 2014 IPO, with trading set to begin Friday.

- **Meta confirms its in-house "Iris" AI chip enters production in September, aiming to double compute capacity to 14 gigawatts next year:** The custom MTIA accelerator was designed with Broadcom and will be fabbed at TSMC, part of a broader push (including a ~$7.3B Samsung 2nm partnership) to cut reliance on Nvidia GPUs. META shares slipped as investors weighed the chip milestone against still-ballooning AI capex.

- **Vista Equity Partners and Quinti Capital make a takeover approach for Criteo at a 50%+ premium:** The private-equity duo's bid values the French ad-tech firm at roughly $3.7 billion on an equity basis; Criteo shares surged as much as 21% on the news, and the company has yet to formally respond.

- **Chip stocks broadly rally as AI capex optimism outweighs geopolitical risk:** A gauge of semiconductor stocks climbed about 4% even as US-Iran tensions escalated overnight, with investors staying focused on the SK Hynix debut and Micron's spending plans; Morgan Stanley notes the "Magnificent Seven" now trade at their smallest valuation premium to the S&P 500 in over a decade, suggesting the AI trade is broadening rather than cooling.

---

## Market Synthesis

Memory and custom silicon are the story today: Micron's $250B US commitment and SK Hynix's blockbuster IPO both signal that chipmakers see years of AI-driven demand still ahead, while Meta's "Iris" chip milestone shows hyperscalers pushing harder to build their own silicon rather than lean solely on Nvidia. Watch **MU** for follow-through after today's jump, and **SK Hynix (HYMTF/ADR)** for its Friday debut — a strong open would be read as a green light for the next wave of large tech IPOs later this year. The Criteo bid is a reminder that private equity still sees value in beaten-down ad-tech names, worth tracking for **CRTO** if a formal deal materializes. Overall, the sector-wide chip rally alongside a narrowing Magnificent Seven valuation premium suggests money is rotating into a broader set of AI winners rather than just the mega-caps — a trend to watch as more earnings and IPOs land later this month.
