# June 12, 2026 — Tech Market Debrief

## Major Deals & Moves (Past 24 Hours)

- **SpaceX completes the largest IPO in history and debuts on Nasdaq:** SpaceX priced its IPO at $135/share (raising ~$75B) and opened trading Friday at $150 under ticker **SPCX**, briefly topping $160–$175 and pushing its market cap above $2T — vaulting it past Tesla as roughly the 7th most valuable US public company. The offering was oversubscribed several times over, with retail investors scrambling for allocations despite some calling the valuation "stupid."

- **OpenAI acquires cloud startup Ona to power persistent AI coding agents:** OpenAI announced it will acquire the German cloud-execution platform Ona, folding its secure, reproducible cloud environments into Codex so AI agents can run multi-step coding tasks for hours or days unattended. Codex usage has grown 400% this year to over 5 million weekly users; deal terms were undisclosed.

- **KKR launches $10B+ "Helix Digital Infrastructure" venture with Nvidia and Vistra:** KKR is starting a new AI infrastructure company backed by more than $10B from founding partners Nvidia, power producer Vistra, and the Kuwait Investment Authority, led by former AWS CEO Adam Selipsky. Helix aims to bundle compute, power, and fiber under one platform — giving Nvidia a direct stake in the data-center buildout and Vistra a major new demand anchor for its gas and nuclear generation.

- **Alibaba bids $1.5B for grocery delivery firm Pupu, escalating fight with Meituan:** Alibaba's offer more than doubles an earlier $600M bid from Sun Art Retail for one of China's last major independent online grocery platforms, intensifying the local-commerce battle with Meituan (which recently bought rival Dingdong Fresh for $717M). The deal could draw Beijing antitrust scrutiny.

- **Space-sector stocks sell off ahead of SpaceX's debut:** Rocket Lab fell about 7%, Redwire dropped more than 6%, and AST SpaceMobile slid 10% in early trading Friday as investors rotated out of smaller space names and into the SpaceX listing, which is absorbing massive liquidity from the sector.

- **Broad market grinds higher on Iran peace hopes and IPO momentum:** The Dow rose 338 points (+0.7%) and the Nasdaq added 0.1% Friday, with stocks on track for a positive week amid reports a deal to lift Iran sanctions and reopen the Strait of Hormuz may be near — easing some of the energy-driven volatility that hit tech names earlier in the week.

---

## Market Synthesis

Today's story is dominated by SpaceX's blockbuster Nasdaq debut, which not only set a record for the largest IPO ever but is also pulling capital and attention away from smaller space and AI names — watch **SPCX** for volatility as the initial pop settles, and smaller space stocks (Rocket Lab, Redwire, AST SpaceMobile) for further weakness. Beyond the IPO, the AI infrastructure land-grab continues unabated: KKR's Helix venture gives **Nvidia** another foothold in physical AI infrastructure alongside power producer **Vistra**, while **OpenAI**'s Ona acquisition shows the agentic-AI coding race is now as much about cloud execution infrastructure as models. Meanwhile, Alibaba's aggressive bid for Pupu signals renewed competitive intensity in Chinese e-commerce, worth watching for **BABA** investors. Combined with easing geopolitical risk from potential Iran sanctions relief, today's tone is broadly risk-on for tech and AI infrastructure plays even as capital rotates toward the SpaceX listing.
