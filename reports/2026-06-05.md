# Tech Deal Debrief — June 5, 2026

## Key Deals & Announcements

- **Anthropic files confidential IPO prospectus with the SEC** — The Claude maker submitted a confidential S-1 on June 1, targeting a valuation above $1 trillion; the company's revenue run-rate hit approximately $47 billion in May — more than 4x its pace from a year ago — and Anthropic says it is on track to post its first quarterly operating profit of ~$559 million on $10.9 billion in Q2 revenue. Amazon and Google, the two largest shareholders, stand to see their stakes repriced at a public-market valuation.

- **SpaceX IPO roadshow enters its final week before a June 12 Nasdaq debut as SPCX** — The company set a roadshow price of $135/share, implying a $1.77 trillion valuation; at $75 billion in proceeds, the listing will more than double the all-time IPO fundraising record (Saudi Aramco's $29.4 billion in 2019) and would make SpaceX the seventh-largest U.S. company by market cap, above Tesla.

- **Paramount gains decisive upper hand in the Warner Bros. Discovery bidding war** — Netflix faces an active DOJ antitrust second request on its $82.7 billion all-cash bid for WBD, while Paramount's $31/share fully-financed cash offer has already cleared its Hart-Scott-Rodino waiting period; WBD's board has flagged Paramount's offer as potentially superior, putting pressure on Netflix to either raise its bid or withdraw.

- **Cyera nears a $300 million funding round led by Evolution Equity Partners at a $12 billion valuation** — the AI-native data-security startup is pricing at roughly 80x ARR, one of the highest multiples in enterprise software this cycle, signaling that investors are still willing to pay premium prices for AI-integrated cybersecurity ahead of what looks like a crowded IPO pipeline.

- **IQM upsizes its SPAC merger PIPE to $146 million ahead of its public listing** — the Finnish quantum hardware company, merging with Real Asset Acquisition Corp., added a commitment from Ilmarinen to increase funding; coming one day after Quantinuum's Nasdaq debut, this marks two quantum computing companies entering public markets in the same week for the first time.

- **Google formally completes its $32 billion acquisition of cloud-security firm Wiz** — after receiving DOJ and European Commission clearance, Wiz is now part of Google Cloud; the deal is the largest cybersecurity acquisition ever and positions Google to compete more directly with Microsoft Defender and AWS Security Hub at the enterprise level.

- **Intel up more than 250% year-to-date on strengthening foundry pipeline** — following the mid-May disclosure of an Apple foundry contract, Intel's committed wafer-start backlog now exceeds $15 billion through 2027, with roughly a third tied to U.S. government and defense customers; Wall Street is beginning to treat Intel Foundry as a genuine third-party fab competitor to TSMC.

## Synthesis

This week's central theme is the long-awaited monetization of AI-era infrastructure bets: Anthropic's IPO filing and SpaceX's imminent listing together represent over $2 trillion in potential new public-market capitalization, and both events will force institutional portfolio managers to reallocate at scale. Watch **AMZN** and **GOOGL** closely — both are major Anthropic investors whose implied stakes will be repriced once the S-1 goes public; a clean IPO above $1 trillion would be a meaningful paper gain for both balance sheets. The WBD bidding war is moving to a resolution, with **WBD** shares likely to swing on any headline about Netflix's next move or a revised Paramount offer. Meanwhile, the back-to-back quantum computing listings (Quantinuum on June 4, IQM via SPAC shortly after) are opening a new investable sector — watch **IONQ** as the most liquid pure-play for sector momentum trading. On semiconductors, **INTC**'s foundry renaissance and **GOOGL**'s completed Wiz deal both point to vertical integration and AI security as the defining capital-allocation priorities of 2026.
