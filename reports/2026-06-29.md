# 2026-06-29 — Tech Deals & Market Movers

---

- **Rocket Lab to acquire Iridium for $8 billion (June 29):** Rocket Lab announced a cash-and-stock deal at $54 per share to buy satellite communications company Iridium, creating a vertically integrated space company that combines Rocket Lab's launch capabilities with Iridium's global L-band network and 500+ partner ecosystem. Iridium surged 21% on the news; Rocket Lab rose 8.3%.

- **Baidu's AI chip unit Kunlunxin targets $50 billion Hong Kong IPO (June 29):** Reports emerged that Baidu's AI chip subsidiary Kunlunxin is targeting a ~$50 billion valuation in a Hong Kong listing, with an unusual structure requiring investors to also commit to purchasing its semiconductors at 3–7× their IPO stake value. Baidu shares jumped 7% on the news, with ByteDance and Tencent already among Kunlunxin customers.

- **Comcast announces major spin-off; Charter surges on SpaceX partnership talks (June 29):** Comcast revealed restructuring plans to spin off parts of its cable business, and separate reports surfaced of Charter Communications exploring a deal with SpaceX. Charter gained 14.5%; Comcast rose 9.8%, signaling major upheaval in traditional cable and the rise of satellite connectivity as a replacement.

- **Apple and Intel partner on U.S.-made chips (June 18):** President Trump announced Apple agreed to work with Intel to design and build chips domestically, pushing Intel shares up more than 9% in a single session and lifting the entire semiconductor sector to new records — a signal that government-backed reshoring deals are moving markets in real time.

- **South Korea launches $576 billion national semiconductor initiative:** Samsung and SK Hynix backed an 800 trillion won plan to build four new domestic fabrication plants, reinforcing South Korea's position in DRAM and advanced logic manufacturing as the geopolitical chip race intensifies between the U.S., China, and Asia.

- **Apple qualifies China's CXMT for iPhone 18 DRAM supply:** Reports confirmed Apple is diversifying its DRAM supply chain by approving Chinese chipmaker CXMT for iPhone 18 memory — a move analysts say is primarily about reducing shortage risk, but one that also signals Apple hedging against both supply disruptions and geopolitical pressure on its existing memory partners.

- **Meta locks in $100 billion AMD GPU deal:** Meta finalized a 6-gigawatt, $100 billion agreement with AMD for custom Instinct MI450 GPUs, including a 160-million-share warrant that could give Meta a 10% stake in AMD — a deal that positions AMD as a credible second source to Nvidia in large-scale AI chip procurement.

---

## What It Means

Today's biggest move is Rocket Lab's $8 billion grab for Iridium — a direct challenge to SpaceX's grip on satellite communications that makes **RKLB** and **IRDM** the clearest near-term trades, while Charter's SpaceX speculation (and Comcast's spin-off) points to a broader cable-to-connectivity shake-up worth watching in **CHTR** and **CMCSA**. On chips, Baidu's Kunlunxin IPO push and Apple's CXMT DRAM deal together signal that China's domestic semiconductor sector is growing fast enough to attract international customers and public market investors — putting U.S. chip restrictions and supply chains back in focus. Meanwhile, the Apple-Intel domestic chip deal and South Korea's $576 billion national investment plan show that government-backed industrial policy is becoming a direct market catalyst: watch **INTC**, **AMAT**, **MU**, and Korean memory proxies as the reshoring and geopolitical chip themes continue driving sector rotation.
