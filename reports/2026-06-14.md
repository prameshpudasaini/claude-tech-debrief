# June 14, 2026 — Tech Market Debrief

## Major Deals & Moves (Past 24 Hours)

- **Nasdaq-100's quarterly rebalance reshuffles ~$1.4T in passive money:** Nasdaq confirmed that Astera Labs (ALAB), CoreWeave (CRWV), Nebius Group (NBIS), Rocket Lab (RKLB), and Teradyne (TER) will join the Nasdaq-100 before the open on June 22, replacing Charter Communications, Cognizant, Insmed, Verisk Analytics, and Zscaler. All five additions popped on the news — Nebius +13%, Rocket Lab +11%, Astera Labs +5% — as index funds begin front-running the rebalance.

- **Adobe's CFO bolts for chipmaker Marvell, overshadowing a beat-and-raise quarter:** Adobe CFO Dan Durn is departing for Marvell Technology effective June 15, with Steve Day stepping in as interim CFO. ADBE shares fell roughly 5% after-hours despite Adobe topping Q2 estimates and raising FY26 guidance, as investors weighed the leadership churn alongside lingering worries about AI's impact on creative-software demand.

- **Google taps Samsung to help build its next AI chip as TSMC capacity tightens:** Google is reportedly in talks with Samsung Electronics to produce the memory I/O die for its 10th-generation TPU (targeted for 2028 mass production), pairing it with a TSMC-built 1.4nm core compute chip. The move marks Samsung's first entry into the logic-die business TSMC has dominated and signals hyperscalers are being pushed toward multi-foundry strategies as AI chip demand outstrips TSMC's advanced-node capacity — one to watch for GOOG, TSM, and Samsung.

- **SpaceX's pending $60B Cursor acquisition keeps the AI M&A pipeline in focus:** With SpaceX's record IPO now closed, attention turns to its option to acquire AI coding startup Cursor for $60B roughly 30 days after listing (around mid-July) — a price that dwarfs Cursor's recent $50B private valuation and would rank among the largest AI acquisitions ever, intensifying scrutiny of how AI labs are funding their cash burn.

- **SPCX holds above $160 as chip stocks see renewed pressure:** SpaceX shares, which closed up 19% near $161 after Friday's record Nasdaq debut, are holding their post-IPO gains and keeping the company's valuation above $2T. Meanwhile, large-cap semiconductor names including ASML, Marvell, and Super Micro Computer faced fresh selling pressure in early trading, a reminder that capital rotation into mega-cap AI listings is still pressuring the broader chip complex.

---

## Market Synthesis

The biggest near-term flow story is the Nasdaq-100's quarterly rebalance: **NBIS**, **RKLB**, **ALAB**, **CRWV**, and **TER** are seeing immediate buying as roughly $1.4T in passive funds prepare to add them by June 22, while **CHTR**, **CTSH**, **INSM**, **VRSK**, and **ZS** face the opposite pressure — a mechanical but real tailwind/headwind worth trading around over the next week. On the strategic side, Google's reported move to bring Samsung into its TPU supply chain underscores that TSMC's capacity constraints are now reshaping AI-chip sourcing decisions industry-wide, a theme likely to keep showing up across **GOOG**, **TSM**, and Korean chip names. Adobe's CFO defection to Marvell is a smaller story but fits the broader pattern of AI-driven talent and leadership competition spilling across software and semiconductors — watch **ADBE** for further executive turnover headlines and **MRVL** for any strategic signal from the hire. Finally, with **SPCX** holding its post-IPO gains while ASML, Marvell, and SMCI wobble, and SpaceX's $60B Cursor option still looming for mid-July, the market continues to reward mega-cap AI/space listings at the expense of mid-tier chip names — a rotation pattern that's likely to persist until the next earnings catalyst.
