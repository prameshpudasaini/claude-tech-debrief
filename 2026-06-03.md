# Tech Deal Debrief — June 3, 2026

## Key Deals & Announcements

- **Anthropic confidentially filed an S-1 with the SEC on June 1**, setting the stage for one of the largest AI IPOs ever attempted. The filing follows the company's late-May Series H raise of $65 billion, pushing its valuation toward $965 billion — just short of a trillion-dollar debut.

- **SpaceX is entering final IPO countdown**, with investor roadshows expected to begin around June 4 and pricing targeted for June 11–12 under ticker SPCX. At an estimated $1.75 trillion valuation and ~$75 billion in projected proceeds, this would shatter Saudi Aramco's 2019 record as the largest IPO in history.

- **AMD and Meta announced a multi-generational 6-gigawatt AI infrastructure deal**, with Meta committing to AMD Instinct MI450-based GPUs and 6th-gen EPYC CPUs for its next wave of AI buildout, with shipments beginning H2 2026. This is a direct competitive shot at Nvidia's near-monopoly on hyperscaler GPU supply.

- **IonQ agreed to acquire chipmaker SkyWater Technology for $1.8 billion**, giving the publicly traded quantum computing firm its own fab to begin testing 200,000-qubit chips by 2028. This is a major vertical integration move in the emerging quantum hardware race.

- **A BlackRock/MGX consortium completed the ~$40 billion acquisition of Aligned Data Centers**, one of the largest private infrastructure deals on record, underscoring the race to lock up AI compute real estate before capacity constraints worsen.

- **Publicis Groupe will acquire data platform LiveRamp (RAMP) for approximately $2.2 billion** ($38.50/share), a premium takeout that effectively removes one of the few independent identity-resolution platforms from the open market.

- **Liftoff Mobile (LFTO) is set to list on NASDAQ on June 4**, offering 19 million shares at $20–$22/share, targeting a ~$3.66 billion valuation in one of the first significant mobile ad-tech IPOs in years.

## Synthesis

The defining theme this week is the AI IPO supercycle arriving at full speed — Anthropic's confidential S-1 and SpaceX's imminent pricing will absorb enormous investor attention and capital, potentially creating short-term rotation pressure on existing large-cap tech names. Watch AMD closely: the Meta 6GW deal is the clearest signal yet that hyperscalers are actively building Nvidia alternatives into their supply chains, which matters for AMD's forward multiples. The IonQ/SkyWater deal signals quantum computing transitioning from pure software/software-adjacent plays to vertically integrated hardware bets — worth tracking for sector rotation. Data infrastructure (BlackRock/Aligned, LiveRamp takeout) continues to attract premium capital, reinforcing that physical and data-layer AI buildout is where smart money is moving beneath the headline AI model names.
